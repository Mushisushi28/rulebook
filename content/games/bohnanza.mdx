## Setup

Shuffle the bean cards and deal 5 cards to each player. Players hold their cards in a strict order — the card drawn first is always at the front of the hand. Place the remaining deck face-down as the draw pile. Give each player 2 fields (field cards) to plant beans in. Each field can hold only one type of bean at a time.

---

## Gameplay

On your turn, you must perform these actions in order:

**1. Plant the first card in your hand.** You must plant the frontmost card in your field (no choice). If you have no matching field, you must harvest an existing field first to make room.

**2. (Optional) Plant the second card in your hand.** You may also plant the second card.

**3. Draw 2 cards face-up and trade.** Draw 2 cards face-up from the deck. You may trade these cards with any other player, or donate them. After trading, any leftover face-up cards you kept must be planted.

**4. Draw 3 cards into your hand.** Add them to the back of your hand in the order drawn — do not rearrange.

**Harvesting:** When you harvest a field, you earn 1 coin for every N beans in that field (the conversion rate is printed on each bean card type). Harvesting a field with only 1 bean may earn 0 coins.

---

## Key Rule

You may NEVER rearrange the cards in your hand. The order they arrived in is the order they must be planted. This constraint makes trading essential.

---

## Winning

The player with the most coins at the end of the game wins. The game ends when the draw pile runs out for the third time.

---

## Tips

- Do not wait too long to harvest. Waiting for perfect sets means you may be forced to harvest early with less income.
- Trade aggressively. Giving away cards you cannot use often nets you back something valuable.
- A third field can be purchased mid-game for 3 coins — consider this if you consistently struggle with two fields.
