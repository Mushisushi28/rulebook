## Setup

Place the round Crokinole board flat on a table. The board has a circular playing surface with scoring rings (20, 15, 10, and 5 points outward from center) and a center hole worth 20 points. Eight pegs surround the inner circle as obstacles.

**2 players:** Sit opposite each other. Each player receives 12 discs of their color.
**4 players:** Play in teams of 2, partners sitting across the board. Each team member receives 6 discs.

---

## Gameplay

Players alternate turns, shooting one disc per turn. All discs must start from behind the shooter's baseline (the outer edge of their quadrant).

**Basic rules:**
- Flick your disc with a finger from your side of the board.
- Your disc must reach at least the 15-point ring, or it is removed from the board.
- **If an opponent disc is on the board,** your disc must make contact with an opponent disc on this shot. If it does not, your disc is removed.
- **If no opponent discs are on the board,** you may shoot anywhere.

**Knocking:** Any disc that is knocked off the board or out of scoring position is removed. This includes your own disc if you knock it away.

**Center hole:** Any disc that falls into the center hole is placed beside the board as a 20-point disc, not on the board.

---

## Scoring

After all discs are played, tally scores. Only the team (or player) with the higher total in a round scores points equal to the difference. For example, if one side scores 55 and the other 40, the winner scores 15 points for that round.

Play continues in rounds until one player or team reaches 100 points.

---

## Winning

First player or team to reach 100 cumulative points wins.

*Crokinole boards are handmade by many artisan craftspeople. The game originates from 19th-century Ontario, Canada.*
