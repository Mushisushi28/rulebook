## Setup

Distribute secret role envelopes: Liberal (majority), Fascist, and one Hitler. Fascists know each other and know who Hitler is. Hitler only knows they are Hitler (in 5–6 player games Hitler sees the lone Fascist). Shuffle the Policy deck (6 Liberal, 11 Fascist tiles). Elect a first Presidential Candidate randomly.

---

## Election Phase

The Presidential Candidate nominates a Chancellor. All players vote Ja! or Nein! simultaneously. Majority Ja! = government elected. If rejected, the Election Tracker advances (3 consecutive failures = top policy tile is enacted automatically).

---

## Legislative Session

The President draws 3 Policy tiles, discards 1 face-down, passes 2 to the Chancellor. The Chancellor discards 1, enacts the remaining tile. No discussion is allowed during this phase.

**Presidential Powers (unlocked by Fascist policies enacted):**
- Investigate Loyalty, Special Election, Policy Peek, Execution.

---

## Winning

**Liberals win** by enacting 5 Liberal Policies OR killing Hitler.
**Fascists win** by enacting 6 Fascist Policies OR electing Hitler as Chancellor after 3+ Fascist Policies are enacted.

---

## Special Rule

Once 3 Fascist Policies are enacted, if Hitler is elected Chancellor, Fascists win immediately.

---

## Tips

- Watch voting patterns — consistent Nein! votes on Fascist-adjacent governments are suspicious.
- Track policy tiles enacted to infer hand contents mathematically.
- Liberals should not shoot randomly; save execution powers for confirmed Fascists.
