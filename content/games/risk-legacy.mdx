## Setup

Choose 5 factions from the available options (each has unique starting powers and units). Each player selects a faction and takes their matching troops. Draw random starting territory cards; place starting troops. Shuffle the Risk cards. Open the Starting Mission pack and deal missions.

---

## Base Gameplay

Risk Legacy follows classic Risk structure: place armies, attack adjacent territories with dice battles (attacker 1-3 dice vs defender 1-2 dice, ties to defender), capture territories, earn Risk cards. At the start of each turn, receive reinforcements based on territories held and continent bonuses.

**Faction powers:** Each faction begins with 2 unique powers (on stickers applied to faction cards) that bend the rules in their favor: extra dice, retreat mechanics, missile deployment, etc.

---

## Legacy Mechanics

**Winning:** The first player to control their faction HQ city wins the game session. The winner gets to name the winning continent on the board (permanent sticker). HQ locations shift between games.

**Founding cities:** When a player holds a territory at game end, they may found a permanent named city there. Cities provide reinforcement bonuses in all future games.

**Opening envelopes:** Specific game events (first missile used, first player eliminated, etc.) trigger envelope openings containing new rules, powers, stickers, and story content.

**Scars:** Losing players apply negative stickers to territories or factions -- permanent wounds to the world.

**Retiring factions:** After 3 wins, a faction may be retired. They choose a permanent bonus to apply to the board forever.

---

## Winning the Campaign

After 15 games, the player with the most wins is the champion. The board -- named, scarred, citied -- becomes a keepsake of your specific playthrough.

---

## Tips

- Secure a continent early; the bonus armies compound over 15 games.
- HQ locations are secret until revealed -- keep options open.
- Do not squander powerful Legacy cards on routine battles; save them for decisive moments.
