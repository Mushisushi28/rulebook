## Setup

Standard 52-card deck. Partners sit opposite each other (North-South vs East-West). Deal all 52 cards, 13 per player. Players sort hands by suit.

---

## The Auction

Starting with the dealer, players bid in turn. A bid names a number (1–7) and a denomination (Club, Diamond, Heart, Spade, or No Trump), e.g., "1 Heart." The bid promises the partnership will win at least (number + 6) tricks with the named trump or no trump.

Each bid must be higher than the last (higher number, or same number with higher denomination: C < D < H < S < NT). Players may also **Pass**, **Double** (penalty double on opponent's bid), or **Redouble** (after a double).

Bidding ends after three consecutive passes. The final bid is the **contract**. The player from the contract's side who first bid that denomination is the **Declarer**; their partner becomes **Dummy**.

---

## Play

The player to the left of Declarer leads first. Immediately after the first lead, Dummy lays all their cards face-up on the table, organized by suit. Declarer plays both their own hand and Dummy's hand throughout. Dummy cannot suggest plays.

Standard trick-taking: follow suit if able, otherwise play any card. Highest trump wins; otherwise highest card of lead suit wins. Declarer must attempt to fulfill the contract.

---

## Scoring

Scoring is complex with books of tables. Key elements:
- **Making contract:** Points for tricks bid and made (below the line), with bonuses for game (3NT, 4♥/♠, 5♣/♦) and slam (6- or 7-bid contracts).
- **Overtricks:** Small bonus points.
- **Undertricks:** Penalty points to defenders (more if doubled/redoubled or vulnerable).
- **Vulnerability:** Changes point values and penalty amounts.

---

## Winning

In rubber bridge, the first side to win two games (accumulate 100+ below-the-line points) wins the rubber and scores a bonus.

---

## Tips

- Learn standard bidding systems (Standard American, Acol) — bridge is as much about communication as card play.
- Count your high-card points: 4 for Ace, 3 for King, 2 for Queen, 1 for Jack.
- Declarer should plan the play of the entire hand before playing the first card from dummy.
