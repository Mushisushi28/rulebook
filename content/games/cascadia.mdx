## Setup

Arrange the 5 habitat tiles as a starting starter pack for each player. Shuffle the 100 habitat tiles and place face-down in a draw bag. Place the 5 wildlife tokens in their separate bags. Draw 4 tile+token pairs to the central display. Randomly select 1 scoring card per wildlife type (from 3 options each). Each player starts with a starter habitat tile and 1 nature token.

---

## Gameplay

On your turn, take 1 tile+token pair from the 4 available in the central display. Add the habitat tile to your growing wilderness (must be placed adjacent to an existing tile). Place the wildlife token on any already-placed tile that shows that animal as one of its compatible species. If a token cannot legally be placed, discard it.

**Nature tokens:** Spend a nature token to either take any tile + any token from the display (mixing them) or replace all 4 wildlife tokens if they are all the same species (the Overpopulation reset). Earn a nature token when you place a wildlife token on its matching habitat (a tile that shows that animal prominently).

Replenish the display to 4 pairs after your turn.

---

## Scoring

**Habitat scoring:** Score points for your largest contiguous groups of each habitat type (forests, mountains, prairies, rivers, wetlands). The exact scoring table depends on the number of contiguous tiles.

**Wildlife scoring:** Each animal type has its own scoring card. Examples: Salmon score for runs (chains of salmon), Hawks score for isolated hawks not adjacent to other hawks, Bears score for groups of exactly 3.

**Nature tokens:** Score 1 point per unspent nature token.

---

## Winning

Most points wins. Ties broken by most wildlife tokens placed.

---

## Tips

- Balance habitat building with wildlife placement -- both score, and neither should be neglected.
- Scout the wildlife scoring cards before drafting; some require specific arrangements planned from turn 1.
- Nature tokens are flexible wildcards; save them for high-value moments.
