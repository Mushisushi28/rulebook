## Setup

Set up the board with the standard arrangement: each player has 15 checkers on specific points — 2 on the 24-point, 5 on the 13-point, 3 on the 8-point, and 5 on the 6-point (mirror image for each player). Both players move in opposite directions toward their own home board (points 1–6).

---

## How to Play

Roll two dice. Move checkers forward the number of points shown on each die (dice may be used in any order, split across different checkers, or both applied to one checker if the move is legal at each step). With doubles, play each die value twice (four moves total).

**Hitting:** Landing on a point with exactly one opponent checker (a "blot") sends it to the **bar**. A player with checkers on the bar must enter them first, by rolling a number corresponding to an open point in the opponent's home board. A point with 2+ checkers of the same color is "made" and safe from hitting.

**Bearing off:** Once all 15 of your checkers are in your home board (points 1–6), begin bearing them off by rolling and removing checkers from the matching point (or lower if needed).

---

## The Doubling Cube

Before rolling, a player may offer to double the stakes. The opponent accepts (taking the cube, now able to redouble) or declines (conceding the current game value). Starting cube value is 64 (2→4→8…→64).

---

## Winning

First player to bear off all 15 checkers wins. **Gammon:** opponent has borne off no checkers — double the stake. **Backgammon:** opponent has a checker on the bar or in winner's home board — triple the stake.

---

## Tips

- Making key blocking points (5-point, 7-point bar) early is strategically crucial.
- Hit blots aggressively early; be careful when far behind in the race.
- Use the doubling cube as a psychological and mathematical weapon.
