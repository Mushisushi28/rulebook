## Setup

Standard 52-card deck. Players sit opposite their partner. Deal all 52 cards, 13 to each player. Players sort their hands.

---

## Bidding

Starting left of dealer, each player bids the number of tricks (0–13) they expect to win. Bids are added to form a team bid. **Nil bid:** A player may bid 0 (Nil), betting they will take no tricks; worth 100 bonus or -100 penalty. **Blind Nil:** Before seeing cards, bid 0 for 200 bonus or -200 penalty.

---

## How to Play

Play begins left of dealer. Any card may lead on the first trick — **except spades**, which cannot be led until a spade has been played as a trump (spades are "broken") or the leader has only spades left.

**Following suit:** You must follow the lead suit if able. If you cannot, you may play any card including spades. Highest spade wins a trick. Otherwise the highest card of the lead suit wins.

Tricks are won and stacked by that team.

---

## Scoring

After 13 tricks, compare actual tricks taken to bid:

- **Made bid:** 10 × bid. Each trick over bid = 1 "bag" (overtrick).
- **Failed bid:** -10 × bid.
- **10 bags accumulated:** -100 point penalty, bags reset to 0.
- **Nil (made):** +100 / **Nil (failed):** -100 (partner's tricks still count toward team bid).

---

## Winning

First team to reach **500 points** wins. If both teams hit 500 in the same hand, the higher score wins.

---

## Tips

- Count trump carefully and communicate through your bids.
- Never take unnecessary tricks when your partner has already secured the team's bid.
- Nil bids are powerful but require a hand with no high cards — coordinate with your partner.
