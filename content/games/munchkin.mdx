## Setup

Separate the Door deck (tan) from the Treasure deck (yellow). Shuffle each separately. Deal 4 Door cards and 4 Treasure cards to each player. All players start at Level 1 with no class or race. Choose a starting player.

---

## Turn Structure

On your turn:

**1. Kick Down the Door:** Flip the top Door card face-up.
- If it is a monster, you must fight it immediately.
- If it is a curse, apply it now.
- If it is anything else, add it to your hand.

**2. Look for Trouble / Loot the Room:** If you did not fight a monster by kicking the door:
- You may play a monster from your hand to fight (Look for Trouble), or
- Draw a card face-down from the Door deck (Loot the Room).

**3. Charity:** If you have more than 5 cards at end of turn, give extras to the lowest-level player (or discard if you are lowest).

---

## Combat

Your combat strength = your level + bonuses from equipped items + class/race abilities. Compare to monster strength. If you win, gain levels and treasure as listed on the monster card. If you lose or flee (roll 5+ on one die to run away), apply the Bad Stuff on the card.

Other players may:
- **Help you:** Play monster enhancers or items to strengthen the monster (backstabbing).
- **Aid you:** Offer to help fight in exchange for treasure -- must agree before combat resolves.

---

## Levels and Winning

Gain a level by defeating monsters. You cannot bribe your way to Level 10 -- the final level must be won through combat. First player to reach Level 10 wins.

---

## Tips

- Never let another player reach Level 9 unchallenged -- pile on enhancers to slow them down.
- Class and race cards amplify your power dramatically; equip them early.
- Negotiate during combat: "I help you fight if I get the big item" is valid and encouraged.
