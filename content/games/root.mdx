## Overview

Root is a game of woodland might and right. Each faction has unique mechanics, victory conditions, and playstyles that interact with the shared board. No two factions play alike.

---

## Setup

Place the forest board. Each faction sets up differently per their boards. Recommended first game: Marquise de Cat + Eyrie Dynasties + Woodland Alliance + Vagabond. Each faction starts at 0 Victory Points.

---

## The Factions

**Marquise de Cat (Industrial):** Controls the board by building Workshops, Sawmills, and Recruiters. Takes many actions but must maintain supply chains. Scores by building structures.

**Eyrie Dynasties (Aristocratic):** Must decree actions at the start of each turn and execute them all — failing to complete the decree causes turmoil and resets their government. Scores by roosting.

**Woodland Alliance (Insurgent):** Spreads sympathy tokens secretly. Sympathy generates outrage when opponents enter sympathized clearings, builds supporters, and eventually sparks revolts to establish bases. Scores by spreading sympathy.

**Vagabond (Adventurer):** A single figure exploring the forest, forming relationships (aid/hostility) with factions, completing quests, and scoring from items and relationships. Scores by questing and aiding.

---

## Shared Turn Structure

Each faction follows Birdsong, Daylight, and Evening in their turn — but the actions in each phase are completely faction-specific per their board.

**Shared rules:**
- Clearings are controlled by the faction with the most pieces there.
- Battle: attacker rolls 2 dice; higher die = attacker hits, lower = defender hits (minimum 1 hit if initiating). Remove pieces equal to hits received.
- Crafting requires workshops in connected clearings matching the craft cost.
- Movement: move any number of your pieces from one clearing to a connected clearing.

---

## Winning

First faction to reach 30 Victory Points wins immediately (mid-turn). Alternatively, a faction wins immediately if they complete their Dominance card condition after playing it.

---

## Tips

- Learn one faction thoroughly before introducing a new one.
- The Eyrie must plan their decree carefully — turmoil resets all progress.
- The Alliance can snowball if sympathy spreads unchecked.
- The Vagabond wins through relationships and quests, not combat.
