## Overview

Pandemic: State of Emergency requires the base Pandemic game to play. It introduces three new modules: Emergency Events, Hinterlands, and the Superbug.

---

## Module 1: Emergency Events

A new Emergency Events deck is shuffled into the Epidemic card pile. When drawn, Emergency Events trigger immediate crises: quarantine breakdowns, supply shortages, or global panic that forces players to make difficult mid-game decisions. Some Emergency Events remain in effect until resolved.

---

## Module 2: Hinterland Cities

Two Hinterland cities (small grey circles) are added to the map at the edge of the board. These cities are not on the normal infection path but become active when Hinterland Event cards are drawn. They can act as disease reservoirs that infect adjacent cities without warning.

---

## Module 3: Superbug

One of the four diseases is designated the Superbug. The Superbug cannot be cured by normal means. To win, players must eradicate the Superbug by removing all its cubes from the board (standard eradication procedure: remove all cubes, then the color no longer spreads). This creates a strong asymmetry — three diseases must be cured AND the fourth must be eradicated.

---

## Setup

Combine module components with the base Pandemic setup. Use 1, 2, or all 3 modules simultaneously for varying difficulty.

---

## Winning

Win conditions depend on active modules. With the Superbug module, players must both cure all curable diseases AND eradicate the Superbug. Standard loss conditions (too many outbreaks, out of cubes, player deck depleted) remain in effect.
