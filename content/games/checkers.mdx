## Setup

Place the 8×8 board with a dark square on the lower-left. Each player places 12 pieces on the dark squares of their nearest three rows. The player with dark pieces moves first.

---

## How to Play

Pieces move diagonally forward to an adjacent empty dark square. **Jumping:** If an opponent's piece is on a diagonal-adjacent square and the square beyond it is empty, you must jump over and capture it, removing it from the board. Multiple jumps are mandatory if available.

**Kinging:** When a piece reaches the opponent's back row, it is crowned King (an additional piece placed on top). Kings may move and jump diagonally in all four directions.

**Mandatory capture:** If a jump is available, you must make it. If multiple capture paths exist, you may choose which to take (in standard American Checkers).

---

## Winning

Win by capturing all opponent pieces or leaving them with no legal moves (blocked). A draw may be agreed if neither player can force a win.

---

## Variants

**International Draughts (10×10):** Larger board, 20 pieces each, Kings fly across the board (long-range), mandatory maximum capture.

---

## Tips

- Advance pieces while keeping a strong back-row defense.
- Trade pieces when you have more Kings or a positional advantage.
- Corners and edge positions are often defensively superior.
- Force your opponent into positions where they must give you a capture.
