## Setup

Lay out **25 word cards** in a 5×5 grid. Randomly select a **key card** and insert it into the stand so only the two Spymasters can see it. The key card shows which words belong to **Red**, which to **Blue**, which are neutral (bystanders), and which is the **Assassin**.

The team whose color appears in the **four corners** of the key card goes first (they have 9 agents instead of 8).

Each team selects one player as **Spymaster**. Spymasters sit together on one side of the table.

---

## Turn Structure

Teams alternate turns. On your team's turn:

### Spymaster Gives a Clue

The active Spymaster says exactly **one word** and **one number**:
- The **word** must relate to the meaning of one or more of your team's words.
- The **number** tells your team how many words the clue relates to.

**Clue restrictions:**
- The clue word must not be a current word on the table, a part of a current word, or a direct translation of one.
- You cannot gesture, change tone, or give additional hints.
- You may say "zero" if you want your team to guess without guidance (unlimited guesses).
- You may say "unlimited" if the number is too high to say.

### Operatives Guess

Your team discusses and taps cards one at a time.

**After each tap, one of four things happens:**
1. **Your team's word** — covered with your team's agent card. You may keep guessing.
2. **Neutral word** — covered with bystander card. Your turn ends immediately.
3. **The opposing team's word** — covered with their agent card. Your turn ends immediately (and they get credit for it).
4. **The Assassin** — your team loses immediately.

You **must** make at least 1 guess. You may make up to **(clue number + 1)** total guesses. You may choose to stop guessing early at any point.

**Bonus guess rule:** If your team correctly guesses all words from a previous clue that went partially unanswered, you earned 1 bonus guess for the current clue.

---

## Winning

A team wins by covering all of their agents' words **before** the other team covers theirs.

A team loses immediately if an operative taps the Assassin card.

If there are no remaining words for a team but it is the other team's turn, the active team wins by uncovering the last of their words.

---

## Two-Player / Cooperative Variant

Both players are on the same team against the clock. You have a deck of double agent cards: start with a set number of rounds and try to guess all your team's words before running out.
