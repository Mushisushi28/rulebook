## Setup

Place the 19 terrain hexes randomly (or per the beginner layout) to form the island. Place a number token on each terrain hex except the desert. Place the robber on the desert hex. Each player chooses a color and takes 5 settlements, 4 cities, and 15 roads. Each player places **2 settlements** and **2 roads** in a snake-draft order (1st–2nd–3rd–4th–4th–3rd–2nd–1st). Each player collects 1 resource for each terrain hex adjacent to their second settlement.

---

## Resources

Catan has **5 resource types**, produced by terrain hexes:
- **Lumber** — Forest
- **Brick** — Hills
- **Wool** — Pasture
- **Grain** — Fields
- **Ore** — Mountains

The **Desert** produces nothing and starts with the Robber.

---

## Turn Structure

On your turn, perform these steps in order:

### 1. Roll Dice
Roll both dice. **All players** collect resources for each settlement or city adjacent to a terrain hex whose number matches the roll.

- A **settlement** earns 1 resource card from each adjacent hex.
- A **city** earns 2 resource cards from each adjacent hex.

**Rolled a 7:** No resources are produced. Any player holding more than 7 resource cards must discard half (rounded down). Then the active player **moves the Robber** to any terrain hex and steals 1 random resource from a player with a settlement/city adjacent to that hex. The robber blocks production on its hex until moved again.

### 2. Trade
You may trade resources in two ways:

**Domestic Trade:** Offer any combination of resources to other players. Both parties must agree. You cannot trade with the bank on other players' behalf.

**Maritime Trade:** Trade with the bank at these rates:
- **4:1** — Any 4 identical resources for 1 of your choice (default)
- **3:1** — Any 3 identical resources for 1 of your choice (requires a 3:1 harbor)
- **2:1** — 2 of a specific resource for 1 of your choice (requires the matching 2:1 harbor)

### 3. Build
Spend resources to build roads, settlements, cities, or development cards. All buildings must be placed from your supply (limited pieces).

**Building Costs:**
- **Road** — 1 Lumber + 1 Brick
- **Settlement** — 1 Lumber + 1 Brick + 1 Wool + 1 Grain
- **City** (upgrades a settlement) — 2 Grain + 3 Ore
- **Development Card** — 1 Wool + 1 Grain + 1 Ore

**Placement Rules:**
- Roads must connect to your own roads, settlements, or cities.
- Settlements must be placed on an unoccupied intersection **at least 2 road lengths away** from any other settlement or city (the Distance Rule).
- Cities replace one of your existing settlements.

---

## Development Cards

Draw from the shuffled development card deck. You **cannot play a development card on the same turn you bought it**.

- **Knight (14):** Move the Robber and steal a resource (same as rolling a 7, but no forced discard).
- **Progress Cards (6):** Road Building (place 2 free roads), Year of Plenty (take any 2 resources from the bank), Monopoly (name a resource; all players give you all cards of that type).
- **Victory Point Cards (5):** Count immediately toward your 10 VP goal.

---

## Special Cards

**Largest Army:** Awarded to the first player who plays 3 Knight cards. Worth **2 VP**. Can be stolen by another player who plays more Knights.

**Longest Road:** Awarded to the first player to build a continuous road of at least **5 segments**. Worth **2 VP**. Can be stolen if another player builds a longer road.

---

## Victory Points

| Item | VP |
|---|---|
| Settlement | 1 |
| City | 2 |
| Longest Road | 2 |
| Largest Army | 2 |
| Victory Point Card | 1 |

---

## Winning

The first player to reach **10 Victory Points** on their turn wins immediately. Victory points from development cards are revealed at the moment of winning.
