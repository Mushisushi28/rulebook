## Setup

Choose a menu of cards using the game board. Standard setup: use the recommended menus in the rulebook for your player count. Place the chosen card types in separate piles and shuffle them together to form the draw deck. Deal cards to each player based on player count (see the chart on the board). Place the scoring tokens and pudding tokens nearby.

**Recommended first-game menu:** Nigiri, Maki Rolls, Tempura, Sashimi, Dumplings, Eel, Tofu, Wasabi, Chopsticks, Pudding.

---

## Gameplay

Sushi Go Party! is played over 3 rounds of card drafting.

Each player looks at their hand and secretly selects one card, then places it face-down. All players reveal simultaneously, then pass their remaining hand to the left. Play continues until all cards are played.

**Scoring at end of each round:**

- **Nigiri:** 1 pt (egg), 2 pts (salmon), 3 pts (squid). Triple value when placed on Wasabi.
- **Maki Rolls:** The player with the most maki icons earns 6 pts; second-most earns 3 pts.
- **Tempura:** 2 cards = 5 pts.
- **Sashimi:** 3 cards = 10 pts.
- **Dumplings:** 1 card = 1, 2 = 3, 3 = 6, 4 = 10, 5 = 15 pts.
- **Eel:** 1 card = -3 pts. 2+ cards = +7 pts.
- **Tofu:** 1 card = 2 pts. 2 cards = 6 pts. 3+ cards = 0 pts.

**Puddings** are kept face-up through all 3 rounds and scored only at the end of the game.

---

## Winning

After 3 rounds, score puddings: player with the most earns 6 pts, player with fewest loses 6 pts (skipped in 2-player). The player with the most points wins.

---

## Menu Variants

The game includes 20 distinct card types across categories (Roll, Appetizer, Special, Dessert). Creating custom menus is a core feature — mix and match to change the game dramatically.
