## Setup

Choose board size (3×3 to 8×8; 5×5recommended for beginners). Each player takes their color pieces: flat stones, standing stones (Walls), and one Capstone. White places one flat stone first; then Black places one flat stone of their choosing. Play then proceeds normally with White going first.

**Piece counts (5×5):** 21 flat stones + 1 capstone per player.

---

## Gameplay

On your turn, do one of two things:

**Place a piece:** Put any piece from your reserve onto any empty square:
- **Flat stone** — placed flat, contributes to your road.
- **Wall (standing stone)** — placed upright, blocks roads but cannot be part of one.
- **Capstone** — the most powerful piece; can flatten Walls (turning them into flat stones under the Capstone).

**Move a stack:** Pick up a stack you control (top piece is yours) and move it orthogonally. You may carry up to as many pieces as the board size number. Drop at least one piece on each square you pass through (including the final square). You cannot pass over Walls or Capstones, but your Capstone can flatten a single Wall as its final move.

**Control:** You control a stack if your piece is on top.

---

## Winning

**Road win:** Connect your two opposite edges with a continuous road of flat stones or capstones you control. Diagonals do not count.

**Flat win:** If the board fills completely or one player runs out of pieces, count flat stones you control on top of all stacks. Most flats wins. Ties go to the player who did not trigger the end condition.

---

## Tips

- Walls disrupt opponent roads but are vulnerable to Capstones.
- Moving stacks can capture opponent pieces and flip control.
- Controlling the center provides road-building flexibility.
