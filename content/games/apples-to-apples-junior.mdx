## Setup

Separate the red Apple cards (nouns and phrases) from the green Apple cards (adjectives/descriptions). Shuffle each deck separately and place them face-down in the center of the table. Deal 5 red Apple cards to each player. Choose a judge for the first round.

---

## Gameplay

The judge flips over the top green Apple card and reads it aloud (examples: "Funny," "Brave," "Delicious," "Ancient").

All other players look at their 5 red cards and choose the one they think best matches — or most entertainingly connects to — the green card. Players place their chosen red card face-down in the center.

The judge shuffles the face-down red cards, then turns them over and reads each one aloud. The judge picks the red card they like best, for any reason. The player who submitted the winning red card keeps the green Apple card as a point.

Everyone draws back up to 5 red cards. The next player clockwise becomes the new judge.

---

## Strategy

- Know your judge. Some judges reward the obvious match; others reward the funniest or most unexpected card.
- Do not throw away obviously strong cards just because the current round does not suit them — save powerhouse cards for a judge likely to appreciate them.
- Context is everything. The same red card can win or lose depending on who the judge is.

---

## Winning

The first player to collect a set number of green Apple cards wins:

| Players | Green Cards to Win |
|---|---|
| 4 | 8 |
| 5 | 7 |
| 6 | 6 |
| 7 | 5 |
| 8+ | 4 |

---

## Notes

Apples to Apples Junior uses age-appropriate cards without the pop-culture references or adult humor of the original version, making it suitable for family play from about age 9 onward.
