## Setup

Place the board in the center of the table. The 17 letter tiles (excluding vowels and uncommon letters Q, X, Z) start in the center lane of the board. Divide players into two teams. One player serves as timekeeper. Set the sand timer nearby.

---

## Gameplay

Teams alternate turns. On your team's turn, flip a category card and read the category aloud (examples: "Things at a birthday party," "Types of weather," "Jobs that require a uniform").

Flip the sand timer. Your team has 30 seconds to shout out a word that fits the category. The word must contain at least one of the contested letters still on the board.

Once your team agrees on a word, move each letter tile that appears in that word one space toward your team's side. If a letter appears twice in the word, it moves two spaces.

**Claiming a letter:** When a letter tile reaches the edge of your team's side, it is claimed and removed from the board. Claimed letters count as permanent points.

---

## Strategy

- Longer words are better — each occurrence of a letter in the word moves the tile one space, so a word with double letters can be powerful.
- Focus on letters near your opponent's edge — pulling them back to center and then toward your side can swing the game.
- Under time pressure, a short word that moves your best letter beats a long word you cannot think of.

---

## Winning

The first team to claim 8 letter tiles wins. If time runs out before either team reaches 8, the team with more claimed letters wins.

---

## Variants

**Individual play:** Play 1v1 with each player controlling one side. Categories are drawn from the deck and each player shouts answers that contain contested letters.

**Junior rules:** Allow more time per turn (60 seconds) and exclude the hardest categories.
