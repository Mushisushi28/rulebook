## Setup

Each player takes a game board with 24 flip-down character faces. Each player secretly draws one Character card from the shuffled deck and keeps it hidden. Both players make sure all 24 characters on their board are standing (face-up).

---

## Gameplay

Players alternate asking one yes/no question per turn about the mystery character physical appearance. Examples: "Does your person have blue eyes?" "Is your person wearing a hat?" "Does your person have a beard?"

The opponent answers "Yes" or "No." After receiving the answer, you flip down (eliminate) all characters that cannot match that description.

You may attempt a guess at any time instead of asking a question: "Is your person Felipe?" If correct, you win. If incorrect, you lose immediately.

---

## Winning

The first player to correctly identify the opponent mystery character wins. Because an incorrect guess means an immediate loss, do not guess until you are confident or have narrowed it down to one or two remaining faces.

---

## Tips

- Ask questions that split the remaining characters evenly -- binary features like "Does your person have glasses?" eliminate roughly half the board.
- Track what you have already asked to avoid redundant questions.
- Hair color, eye color, and facial hair are the most differentiating features early in the game.
