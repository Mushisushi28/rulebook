## Setup

Assign roles: one Forensic Scientist, one Murderer (secretly), optionally one Accomplice and Witness. All other players are Investigators. The Murderer secretly selects one of their Scene cards as the murder weapon and one as the key evidence, and tells the Forensic Scientist privately.

Deal each player 4 Clue cards (murder weapons/evidence items). Each player also receives 4 Means cards (scene details).

---

## Gameplay

**The Forensic Scientist** cannot speak during the game. They may only communicate by placing one of their Solution tiles on a Scenario board space, pointing to clues without gesturing beyond placing tiles.

**Each round:**
1. The Forensic Scientist places their 6 Solution tiles on the Scenario board categories (e.g., Location, Cause of Death, Reason, Method of Disposal).
2. Investigators discuss what the tiles mean in the context of the clue categories and scene details they hold.
3. After open discussion, any Investigator may accuse: point to one player and identify their weapon card AND evidence card. If correct, Investigators win.
4. Each Investigator has one accusation attempt per game. If all accusation attempts are used and none are correct, the Murderer wins.

**Forensic Scientist rotation:** After each round of placement, Investigators may ask the Forensic Scientist one yes/no question by gesture — they can only shake or nod.

**Murderer/Accomplice deception:** Killers participate in discussion and may misdirect, deny, and otherwise deceive — as long as they do not reveal their own role.

---

## Winning

- **Investigators win:** One correct accusation before all attempts are spent.
- **Murderer wins:** All accusation attempts fail, OR the Witness is correctly accused (if the Witness role is in play).

---

## Tips

- Forensic Scientists should place tiles that narrow possibilities, not just confirm one option.
- Killers should accuse others confidently to scatter suspicion.
- Pay attention to which players seem most eager to redirect discussion.
