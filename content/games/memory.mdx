## Setup

Shuffle all cards and lay them face-down in a grid (4x8, 6x6, or similar depending on card count). Players decide who goes first.

---

## Gameplay

On your turn, flip any two cards face-up one at a time so all players can see them.

**If they match:** Keep the pair. Take another turn.

**If they do not match:** Flip both cards face-down in their original positions and your turn ends.

The key skill is memorizing where specific cards are as they are revealed each turn, so you can make matches on future turns.

---

## Winning

The game ends when all pairs have been collected. The player with the most pairs wins. Ties are common and can be broken by whoever collected the last pair.

---

## Variants

**Themed decks:** Use photo cards (animals, vehicles, foods) instead of abstract images for younger children.

**Timed:** Flip the entire grid face-up for 30 seconds, then flip back and begin play.

**Solitaire:** Race against yourself to clear the board as quickly as possible.

---

## Tips

- Scan the whole grid before memorizing positions; clusters of similar-looking cards in different positions trip up new players.
- When playing with young children, use fewer cards (20-24) to keep games short.
- Deliberately remembering positions your opponents flip is as important as remembering your own discoveries.
