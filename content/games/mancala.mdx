## Setup

Place the board between players so each has a row of 6 small cups (pits) and a large scoring cup (Mancala/store) on their right. Place 4 seeds in each of the 12 small pits (48 seeds total). Each player owns the row of pits closest to them and the Mancala on their right.

---

## How to Play

The younger player goes first. On your turn:

1. Pick up **all seeds** from any one of your own pits.
2. **Sow** the seeds one-by-one into each pit counter-clockwise — including your own Mancala but **skipping your opponent's Mancala**.
3. If the last seed lands in your **Mancala**, take another turn.
4. If the last seed lands in an **empty pit on your side**, capture that seed plus all seeds in the directly opposite pit on your opponent's side — place them all in your Mancala.

---

## Winning

The game ends when all six pits on one side are empty. The other player collects all remaining seeds from their side into their Mancala. The player with the most seeds in their Mancala wins.

---

## Variants

**Kalah** — The most common Western variant described above.
**Oware/Awale** — Traditional African variant with different capture rules: you capture when a sow ends with 2 or 3 seeds in an opponent pit.

---

## Tips

- Aim for free turns by landing your last seed in your Mancala.
- Chain free turns to dramatically outpace your opponent in seed collection.
- Be careful not to empty your row prematurely — it ends the game, often benefiting your opponent.
