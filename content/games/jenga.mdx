## Setup

Stack the 54 wooden blocks into a tower of 18 layers with 3 blocks per layer. Alternate the direction of each layer 90 degrees from the one below. The tower should be straight and even before play begins.

---

## Gameplay

The player who built the tower goes last. On your turn, use one hand at a time to remove any one block from any level below the top three completed layers. You may tap or nudge blocks to test their stability before committing to a removal.

Once removed, place the block on top of the tower to complete the topmost layer (or start a new layer if the top is full). A layer is complete when it has 3 blocks.

Your turn ends when the next player touches the tower or when 10 seconds have elapsed after you remove your hand.

---

## Winning

The player who causes the tower to fall loses. The winner is the last player to successfully remove and replace a block before the collapse.

---

## Tips

- Tap blocks gently to find loose ones -- tight blocks are risky to remove.
- Blocks at the edges of a layer are usually easier to remove than center blocks (which may be load-bearing).
- Never remove the last block from a three-block layer if it leaves the layer above unsupported on one side.
- Slow and steady always beats fast and reckless.
