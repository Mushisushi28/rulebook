## Setup

Use a 48-card Pinochle deck (Ace, 10, King, Queen, Jack, 9 in each of 4 suits, duplicated). Standard competitive form is 2 vs 2 partnership play. Deal 12 cards to each player. The highest-ranked suit chosen after bidding becomes trump.

---

## Card Rank and Values

Rank (high to low): Ace, 10, King, Queen, Jack, 9. In trick points: Aces worth 11, Tens worth 10, Kings worth 4, Queens worth 3, Jacks worth 2, Nines worth 0. Last trick is worth 10 points. Total trick points: 250.

---

## Bidding

Starting left of the dealer, players bid the minimum number of points they believe their side can score (tricks + meld). Minimum bid is typically 250. Bidding continues clockwise; players may pass. The highest bidder wins the right to name trump.

---

## Melding

After bidding, each player lays down meld combinations from their hand face-up to score points. Common melds:

- **Pinochle:** Jack of Diamonds + Queen of Spades = 40 pts (double Pinochle = 300)
- **Run (Flush):** A-10-K-Q-J of trump suit = 150 pts
- **Four Aces (one per suit):** 100 pts
- **Four Kings:** 80 pts
- **Four Queens:** 60 pts
- **Four Jacks:** 40 pts
- **Marriage (K+Q same suit):** 20 pts (40 if trump)

Sum all melds for each partnership. Return melds to hand.

---

## Trick Play

Eldest hand leads first. Players must follow suit if able. If void in led suit, must play trump if able. Winning trick must beat the current high card when able. Take 10 tricks per hand; last trick worth extra.

---

## Winning

The bidding team must score at least their bid (melds + tricks) or "go set" (lose points equal to their bid). The opposing team always counts what they earn. First team to reach the target score (typically 1,500) wins.

---

## Tips

- Bid conservatively; going set is costly.
- Count the meld points before bidding -- a strong meld hand can carry a weak trick-taking hand.
- In partnerships, signal your strength via bid amounts.
