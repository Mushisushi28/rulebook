## Setup

Unfold the board (North America by default). Place **Train Car cards** and **Destination Tickets** in separate draw piles. Place **45 colored Train Car cards** face-up in a row of 5 (the face-up display).

Each player takes:
- **45 Train Car pieces** in their color
- **4 Train Car cards** (drawn from the deck)
- **3 Destination Ticket cards** — keep at least 2, return unused to the bottom of the deck

Place **1 Locomotive card** face-up in the bonus card area. Highest die roll goes first.

---

## Train Car Cards

There are **8 colored suits** plus **Locomotives** (wild). Colors correspond to routes on the board. You need a set of matching cards to claim a route.

**Face-up display:** At the start of your turn, there are always 5 face-up cards. Drawing from this display is one action option.

**Locomotives:** Count as any color. If 3 or more Locomotives appear face-up simultaneously, discard all 5 and redeal.

---

## Turn Structure

On your turn, choose **exactly one** of three actions:

### 1. Draw Train Car Cards
Draw 2 Train Car cards. Each card may come from:
- The **face-up display** — you see what you get
- The **face-down deck** — blind draw

**Locomotive exception:** Taking a face-up Locomotive counts as **both draws** (it is your only action). You may never take a face-down Locomotive as your second draw if you took a face-up colored card first.

After drawing, refill the face-up display from the deck.

### 2. Claim a Route
Place Train Car pieces on an unoccupied route by discarding the required number of matching colored cards.

**Route requirements:**
- Gray routes accept any single color.
- Colored routes require that specific color (or Locomotives as wild).
- The number on the route equals how many cards you must discard.
- Double routes (two parallel tracks between cities): in 2–3 player games, only one route of a double may be claimed. In 4–5 player games, both routes are available but one player cannot claim both.

### 3. Draw Destination Tickets
Draw **3 Destination Ticket cards**. Keep at least 1. Return any you don't want to the bottom of the deck.

---

## Destination Tickets

Each ticket shows two cities and a point value. If you complete the route connecting those cities at game end, you **earn** those points. If you don't complete it, you **lose** those points.

---

## Scoring

**Route lengths earn points during the game:**
| Route Length | Points |
|---|---|
| 1 | 1 |
| 2 | 2 |
| 3 | 4 |
| 4 | 7 |
| 5 | 10 |
| 6 | 15 |

**End-game bonuses:**
- **Destination Tickets:** +/– face value for each completed/incomplete ticket.
- **Longest Continuous Path:** The player with the longest single connected path of train cars earns **10 bonus points**. Branches do not count — only the longest single path through your network.

---

## Game End

The game ends after the turn in which a player has **2 or fewer Train Car pieces** remaining. All players (including the player who triggered it) take one final turn, then scoring occurs.

---

## Winning

Highest score wins. Tiebreaker: most completed Destination Tickets.
