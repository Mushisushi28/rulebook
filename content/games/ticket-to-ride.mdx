## Setup

Lay out the board. Deal 4 train cards to each player. Deal 3 Destination Ticket cards to each player; each must keep at least 2 (return any extras face-down). Each player takes their 45 colored train cars and matching score marker, placed on 1 on the score track.

---

## Turn Structure

On your turn, choose one of three actions:

**1. Draw Train Cards:** Draw 2 cards from the face-up display or the face-down deck. Locomotives (wild) count as one draw if taken face-up. May draw any combination.

**2. Claim a Route:** Spend the exact number of matching color train cards shown on the route and place your train cars on those spaces. Locomotives may substitute for any color. Score points immediately:
- 1 train = 1 pt, 2 = 2 pts, 3 = 4 pts, 4 = 7 pts, 5 = 10 pts, 6 = 15 pts

**3. Draw Destination Tickets:** Draw 3 Destination Ticket cards, keep at least 1. Destination tickets show two cities and a point value — connect them for bonus points, fail to connect and subtract those points.

---

## End Game

When any player has 0, 1, or 2 train cars remaining, each player (including that player) gets one final turn. Then score:
- Destination tickets completed: + points shown
- Destination tickets incomplete: - points shown
- **Longest Continuous Path bonus:** 10 points to the player with the longest connected railway.

---

## Winning

The player with the highest total score wins.

---

## Tips

- Keep your destinations secret — revealing your cities lets opponents block key routes.
- Claim long routes early; short routes can be found at game end.
- Watch opponents' building patterns to anticipate blocks.
