## Setup

Each player chooses a charter (plot of land on the board) and a character. Open only the sealed components instructed at setup. Each player receives starting resources and a character card. Place 1 worker on the designated starting space.

---

## Gameplay

Charterstone uses worker placement mechanics: place a worker on a building space to take its action (gain resources, draw cards, build new structures, etc.). Workers already placed block others.

**Crates:** Many actions involve opening numbered crates (sealed components). When you open a crate, you find a building, rule card, or story content. Sticker the new building onto the board -- it becomes a permanent fixture for the rest of the campaign.

**Building:** Pay resources to construct buildings in your charter using building cards acquired during play. Each building adds a new action space to the board.

**Coins and VP:** Earn coins and victory point tokens throughout the game. Convert coins to VP at end of game.

---

## Legacy Elements

After each game session, complete the Legacy Book prompts. Answer story questions, apply sticker consequences, unlock new sealed components, and advance the village narrative. Your choices affect future game sessions in ways that diverge from other groups playing the same box.

Characters gain perks (stickered permanently to character cards). Buildings can be upgraded or destroyed. The village grows over 12 sessions into a unique artifact specific to your group.

---

## Winning

Within each game session, most VP wins. Across the full 12-game campaign, track cumulative scores; the campaign winner is determined at the final game end.

---

## Tips

- Invest in your own charter buildings early -- they give you exclusive action advantage.
- Crate openings are exciting but expensive; plan your resources around them.
- Legacy decisions that feel minor now compound into major advantages or disadvantages by game 8+.
