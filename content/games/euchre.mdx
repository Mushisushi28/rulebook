## Setup

Use a 24-card deck: 9 through Ace of all four suits. Deal 5 cards to each player in two rounds (2-3 or 3-2). Turn up the remaining card to propose a trump suit.

---

## Bidding

**Round 1:** Starting left of dealer, each player may order the dealer to pick up the face-up card (making its suit trump) or pass. If ordered up, the dealer takes the card and discards one. The dealer's partner may "go alone."

**Round 2:** If all four players pass in Round 1, the face-up card is turned down. Starting left of dealer again, each player may name a trump suit (must be different from the turned-down suit) or pass. The dealer **must** name trump if it comes back to them (stuck the dealer rule).

---

## How to Play

The player left of the dealer leads first. Players must follow lead suit if able. Highest trump wins; otherwise highest card of lead suit wins.

**Trump hierarchy (highest to lowest):**
1. Jack of trump (Right Bower)
2. Jack of same-color non-trump suit (Left Bower — counts as trump)
3. Ace, King, Queen, 10, 9 of trump

---

## Scoring

- **Making your bid:** Makers win 3–4 tricks = 1 point; all 5 tricks (march) = 2 points.
- **Euchre:** Defenders win 3+ tricks = 2 points for defenders.
- **Going alone and succeeding:** All 5 tricks = 4 points; 3–4 tricks = 1 point.

---

## Winning

First team to 10 points wins.

---

## Tips

- Order up trump only if your hand can reliably win 3 tricks.
- The Left Bower is trump — don't accidentally void yourself.
- Going alone is most effective with 3+ trump cards and an off-suit Ace.
