## Setup

Use the world-map game board. Set up territories with the historically based starting unit positions for five powers: USA, UK, USSR (Allies) vs Germany, Japan (Axis). Distribute starting Industrial Production Certificates (IPCs) per the rulebook. Each power controls a set of territories and collects IPCs based on territory values.

---

## Turn Order

Each power takes a complete turn in fixed order: USSR, Germany, UK, Japan, USA (varies by edition). One turn per player per round.

**Turn phases:**
1. **Purchase Units:** Spend IPCs to order units (infantry, tanks, fighters, bombers, ships, submarines, transports, factories).
2. **Combat Move:** Move units into enemy-occupied territories or seas for battle.
3. **Conduct Combat:** Roll dice for attacking and defending units. Combat continues until one side is eliminated or attacker retreats. Dice based on unit type (infantry attack on 1, defend on 2, etc.).
4. **Non-Combat Move:** Move remaining units into friendly territories.
5. **Mobilize Units:** Place purchased units at factories.
6. **Collect Income:** Gain IPCs equal to the value of all territories you control.

---

## Winning

**Axis win:** Capture 2 of 3 Allied capitals (London, Moscow, Washington/Ottawa).
**Allied win:** Capture Berlin and Tokyo.

---

## Tips

- Concentrate force; spread too thin and you'll lose territories faster than you gain them.
- Navy controls sea lanes needed to deliver troops — submarines block convoy routes.
- Industrial output determines the long-term winner; preserve your factories.
