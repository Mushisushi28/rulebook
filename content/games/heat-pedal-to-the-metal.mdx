## Setup

Choose a circuit (the game includes multiple tracks). Set up the starting grid by player count. Shuffle each player personal 10-card deck. Deal each player a starting hand of cards. Place Heat cards (special limit-pushers) and Cooldown cards in separate piles. Choose whether to use weather, legend cars, or championship rules.

---

## Turn Structure

All players simultaneously reveal and play cards. On your turn:

**1. Shift gear:** Move your gear pawn up or down 1 step, or stay (gears 1-6 determine your card draw for next turn). High gears = more cards = more speed.

**2. Play speed cards:** Play cards from your hand. The sum of all played cards is your movement. You must play at least 1 card.

**3. Move:** Advance your car the number of spaces shown. If you overshoot a corner limit (the number printed on the road), you must pay the excess in Heat cards (drawn from your engine into your hand -- deadweight). If you run out of Heat cards, you spin out.

**4. Cooldown or discard:** At the end of your turn, discard played cards. Slipstreaming (following closely behind another car) earns bonus movement.

---

## Corners and Speed Limits

Each corner shows the maximum speed you can exit at. Count how many spaces your movement takes you through or past the corner. Any excess requires 1 Heat per excess space. Managing Heat is the core skill -- run too hot and your hand fills with Heat cards that cannot be played for speed.

---

## Winning

First car to cross the finish line after all laps wins. In Championship mode, total points across multiple races determine the winner.

---

## Tips

- High gears give more card draw but generate more Heat when cornering hard.
- Boost at the right moment: a long straight after a tight corner lets you spend accumulated Heat efficiently.
- Draft behind leaders on straights for free speed.
