## Setup

Five Crowns uses a double deck of 116 cards with five suits: Spades, Clubs, Hearts, Diamonds, and Stars. Cards run from 3 to King, plus Jokers. Shuffle the full deck. The game is played over 11 rounds.

**Round 1:** Deal 3 cards to each player. Place one card face-up as the discard pile. The remaining cards form the draw pile.

Each subsequent round, deal one more card than the previous round (4 cards in round 2, 5 in round 3, up to 13 in round 11).

**Wild cards change each round:** 3s are wild in round 1, 4s in round 2, 5s in round 3, and so on up to Kings in round 11. Jokers are always wild.

---

## Gameplay

On your turn:
1. Draw the top card from the draw pile or the top discard.
2. Arrange your hand, using wilds if desired.
3. Discard one card face-up on the discard pile.

**Going out:** When your entire hand can be organized into valid books and runs (with no leftover cards), you may go out on your turn after discarding. Announce "Going out!" All other players get one final turn to reduce their hand before scoring.

**Books:** 3 or more cards of the same rank (suits do not matter).
**Runs:** 3 or more consecutive cards of the same suit.
Wild cards substitute for any card in a book or run.

---

## Scoring

After going out, all other players count the face value of remaining cards in their hands:
- 3–10: face value
- Jack = 11, Queen = 12, King = 13, Joker = 50
- Wild cards count as their printed rank value

The player who went out scores 0 for that round. All others score their leftover card values.

---

## Winning

After all 11 rounds, the player with the lowest cumulative score wins.
