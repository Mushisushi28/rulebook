## Setup

Deal hidden role cards: most players are Resistance, a few are Imperial Spies (count varies by player total). Spies see each other; Resistance do not. The Leader token begins with one player.

---

## Round Structure (5 missions)

**1. Team Building:** The Leader proposes a team of players to go on the mission (team size varies by mission and player count, shown on the mission track).

**2. Team Vote:** All players simultaneously vote Approve or Reject (flip cards). Majority approves or rejects. If rejected 5 times consecutively, spies win immediately.

**3. Mission:** Team members secretly play a Success or Fail card. Resistance must play Success; Spies choose either. Reveal and count — **any single Fail card** means the mission fails (except Mission 4 in some setups requires 2 Fails).

**4. Results:** Track success or failure. First side to win 3 missions wins.

**5. Pass Leader:** Rotate Leader clockwise.

---

## Winning

**Resistance:** Complete 3 missions successfully.
**Spies:** Fail 3 missions.

---

## Variants

**Avalon:** Merlin and Percival roles add information layers; Assassin wins if they identify Merlin at game end.

---

## Tips

- Reject teams with players you distrust — forcing more votes reveals voting patterns.
- Spies should sometimes vote for doomed Resistance members' teams to seem trustworthy.
- As Resistance, track who voted for which teams; failed missions narrow the suspect list.
