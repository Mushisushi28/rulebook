## Setup

Lay out the central game board (the time track -- a spiral of 53 spaces). Each player takes a 9x9 personal board and places their time token on space 0. Set up all 33 patches in a circle around the board. Place the neutral pawn to the right of the 2-1 patch. Give each player 5 buttons to start.

---

## Gameplay

The player whose time token is furthest behind on the track takes their turn (ties go to the player on top). On your turn, choose one of two actions:

**Buy a patch:** You may purchase any of the 3 patches immediately clockwise of the neutral pawn. Pay its button cost (from your supply) and time cost (advance your token forward that many spaces on the time track). Place the patch anywhere on your 9x9 board (it may not overlap or extend beyond the grid). Move the neutral pawn to the gap left by the purchased patch. You immediately earn the button income shown on your current quilt board whenever you land on or pass a button icon on the time track.

**Advance and receive buttons:** Move your token to the space just ahead of your opponent token (or past them). Earn 1 button per space moved.

Whenever any player passes a button icon on the time track, they earn buttons equal to the number of button icons currently on their quilt board.

Special 1x1 patches are found on specific time track spaces and can be taken when passed.

---

## Winning

When both tokens reach the end of the time track, the game ends. Players count their buttons and subtract 2 for each empty square on their board. Highest score wins.

---

## Tips

- Patches with high button income pay for themselves over time; prioritize them early.
- Covering the 7x7 bonus square earns an extra 7 points.
- Time cost matters as much as button cost -- slow patches let your opponent take many turns.
