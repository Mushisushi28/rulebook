## Setup

Each player chooses an Explorer character tile and miniature, noting their starting stats (Might, Speed, Sanity, Knowledge — each rated 2-8 on sliding tracks). Place the Entrance Hall tile. Shuffle the three tile stacks (Basement, Ground Floor, Upper Floor). Shuffle Event, Item, and Omen cards into separate decks.

---

## Pre-Haunt Exploration

Players take turns moving through the house, revealing new room tiles when they enter unexplored doorways.

**On your turn:**
1. Move up to your Speed stat in spaces. You may stop early.
2. If entering a new room, draw and place a tile. Follow any symbol: Item (draw item card), Event (draw event card), Omen (draw omen card and roll the Haunt die).

**Events and Items:** Events may raise or lower stats, deal damage, or trigger story text. Items provide ongoing abilities or resources.

**Omen cards:** Omen cards are items with a dark significance. After drawing each Omen, roll 6 dice — if the total is less than the number of Omens drawn so far, the Haunt begins.

---

## The Haunt

When the Haunt triggers, consult the Haunt chart using the current Omen card and the room it was found in. This determines WHICH of the 50 Haunts activates AND which player becomes the Traitor (if any).

The Traitor reads the Traitor tome (secret objectives and special rules). All other players read the Survivors tome (their cooperative objective).

**The Traitor's goal:** Often to kill survivors, complete a ritual, or escape. They may command monsters.

**Survivors' goal:** Varies by Haunt — banish the monster, seal the house, escape, etc.

---

## Winning

The first side to complete their objective wins. Haunts vary enormously — some are combat-focused, others are puzzle-solving races against time.

---

## Tips

- Collect items aggressively pre-Haunt; you may need them against the Traitor.
- High-Sanity characters better resist events that lower Sanity to 0 (elimination).
- The Haunt chart has 50 scenarios — replayability is extremely high.
