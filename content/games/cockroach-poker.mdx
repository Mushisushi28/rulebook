## Setup

Shuffle all 64 pest cards (cockroaches, rats, flies, stink bugs, bats, toads, scorpions, and spiders — 8 cards of each type). Deal all cards evenly to players. Players hold their cards face-down and do not look at them until they want to play one.

---

## Gameplay

On your turn, choose any one card from your hand. Look at it secretly. Pass it face-down to any other player and announce what type of pest it is. Your announcement can be truthful or a lie — your choice.

The receiving player must now:
- **Believe you:** They flip the card face-up. If it matches your claim, they keep it face-up in front of them. If you lied, you take it face-up instead.
- **Call your bluff:** They flip the card face-up. If it does NOT match your claim (you lied), they keep it face-up in front of them. If you told the truth, you take it face-up instead.
- **Pass it on:** Without looking at the card, they peek at it secretly and pass it to another player, making their own claim (same as your claim or a different one). This can continue until someone must accept it.

Once a card is resolved, the player who took it starts the next round.

---

## Losing

A player loses (and the game ends) when they have 4 or more face-up cards of the same type in front of them.

---

## Strategy

- Track what has been claimed and passed. If everyone claims cockroaches, something suspicious is happening.
- Unexpected truths can be more powerful than lies. Saying "this is a scorpion" when you always lie may cause your opponent to call your bluff incorrectly.
- Passing a card is powerful — you can peek and re-lie, building confusion.
