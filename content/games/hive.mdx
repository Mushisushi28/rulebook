## Setup

Each player takes 11 pieces: 1 Queen Bee, 2 Beetles, 2 Grasshoppers, 3 Ants, 2 Spiders. White places first. There is no board -- pieces are placed directly on the table. The playing area expands as pieces are added.

---

## Placement Rules

On your turn, either place a new piece or move an existing piece.

**Placing:** A newly placed piece must touch only your own pieces (except for the very first placement of each color, which establishes the Hive). You may not place a piece touching an opponent piece unless it is the opening move.

**Queen Bee Rule:** You must place your Queen Bee by your 4th turn. You cannot move any pieces until your Queen Bee is in play.

---

## Movement

Each piece type has a unique movement:

**Queen Bee:** Slides 1 space around the Hive.
**Beetle:** Moves 1 space; can climb on top of other pieces (blocking their movement and controlling their color).
**Grasshopper:** Jumps in a straight line over any number of adjacent pieces, landing on the first empty space.
**Ant:** Slides to any space on the outside of the Hive.
**Spider:** Slides exactly 3 spaces around the Hive (no backtracking).

**Freedom of movement:** A piece can slide only if it is not wedged in (both neighbors must not be blocking the slide). **One Hive rule:** A piece may not move if removing it would split the Hive into two disconnected groups.

---

## Winning

The player whose Queen Bee is surrounded on all 6 sides loses. If both Queens are surrounded simultaneously, the game is a draw.

---

## Tips

- Ants are the most powerful pieces; deploy them early to threaten the opponent Queen.
- Beetles can immobilize key opponent pieces by climbing on them.
- Never move a piece that is the only link connecting two parts of the Hive.
