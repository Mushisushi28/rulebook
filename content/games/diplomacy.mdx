## Setup

Use the map of pre-WWI Europe divided into land territories and sea spaces. Each of the 7 great powers (England, France, Germany, Italy, Austria-Hungary, Russia, Turkey) starts with a set number of armies and fleets in home supply centers.

**Supply centers:** 34 starred provinces. A power's unit count equals its supply centers at year-end. Start counts vary by power (3–4 units).

---

## Turn Structure

Each game year has two **move seasons** (Spring, Fall) and a **Winter adjustment phase**.

**Negotiation phase:** Players discuss privately and publicly, forming agreements (all verbal — none binding). Duration is agreed by all players.

**Order phase:** All players write orders simultaneously for every unit and reveal at once.

---

## Unit Orders

- **Move:** Unit attempts to move to an adjacent territory.
- **Hold:** Unit stays; adds defensive strength.
- **Support:** Unit supports an adjacent unit's move or hold (increases attack/defense strength).
- **Convoy (fleets only):** Fleet transports an army across a sea space.

**Resolving orders:** Moves succeed unless blocked by equal or greater opposing strength. Dislodged units must retreat or disband.

---

## Winning

The first player to control **18 supply centers** wins outright. Draws may be agreed by all survivors.

---

## Tips

- No dice — your success depends entirely on diplomacy and your willingness to keep or break promises.
- Never trust a 2-on-1 ally in the late game; you become the next target.
- Stalemate lines (fixed defensive positions) can preserve a draw when you can't win.
