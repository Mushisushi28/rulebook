## Overview

Ticket to Ride: Europe is a standalone version of Ticket to Ride set on a European map. New mechanics include Tunnels (unpredictable extra cost), Ferries (require Locomotives), and Train Stations (let you use one opponent route per city).

---

## Setup

Place the Europe board. Each player takes 45 plastic trains in their color, a score marker (placed on 0), and a reference card. Deal 4 Destination Ticket cards — keep at least 2. Deal 4 Train Cards to each player. Place the remaining Train Cards as a draw pile; flip 5 face-up as the display.

---

## Gameplay

On your turn, take one action:

**Draw Train Cards:** Take 2 cards from the face-up display or draw pile. Face-up Locomotives count as 1 card drawn but are wilds.

**Claim a Route:** Play matching color cards (or Locomotives) equal to the route length and place your trains on it. Score points immediately per route length:
- 1=1, 2=2, 3=4, 4=7, 5=10, 6=15 points.

**Tunnel routes:** When claiming a Tunnel (gray arch track), draw 3 cards from the deck. For each matching card drawn, pay 1 additional card or abandon the claim. Locomotives always match.

**Ferry routes:** Require at least N Locomotives in the played set (shown by locomotive symbols on the route).

**Train Stations:** Pay 1 card (first station), 2 cards (second), 3 cards (third). Place a station in any city. At game end, use 1 opponent route from each stationed city to complete your tickets.

**Draw Destination Tickets:** Draw 3; keep at least 1.

---

## End of Game

When a player has 2 or fewer trains remaining, complete the current round and score:

- Add destination ticket points (positive if completed, negative if not).
- Longest Continuous Path: +10 points (1 player).
- Globetrotter bonus: +15 points to the player with most completed tickets (tie = both earn it).

Highest score wins.
