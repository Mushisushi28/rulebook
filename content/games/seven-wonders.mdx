## Setup

Deal each player a Wonder board (7 ancient wonders, each player gets one side). Shuffle the Age I cards and deal 7 to each player. Set aside any unused cards based on player count. Each player starts with 3 coins.

---

## Three Ages

The game has three Ages (I, II, III), each played with its own card deck. In each Age, players simultaneously choose one card from their hand, play it, and pass their remaining hand to a neighbor (direction alternates by Age). Repeat until 2 cards remain; discard both.

---

## Playing Cards

Cards may be:
- **Brown/Grey (Resources):** Provide raw or manufactured resources. Usually free in Age I, chain-built later.
- **Blue (Civic):** Score victory points immediately.
- **Yellow (Commerce):** Provide coins, reduce resource costs, or give production bonuses.
- **Green (Science):** Score points via sets of symbols (cog, compass, tablet). Collect all three for bonus points; collecting duplicates multiplies scoring.
- **Red (Military):** Add shields to your military strength.
- **Purple (Guilds, Age III only):** Score based on neighbors developments or your own structures.

**Free building:** Some cards list a chain symbol. If you previously built the named card, you may build the chained card for free.

**Wonder stages:** Instead of playing a card, you may use it as material to build the next stage of your Wonder (pay its cost, gain its benefit, discard the played card).

---

## Military

At the end of each Age, compare your shield count with each neighbor. Win: +1 victory (Age I), +3 (Age II), +5 (Age III). Lose: -1 each age.

---

## Winning

After Age III military resolution, sum all points: civic, science, military, Wonders, coins, commerce, guilds. Most points wins.

---

## Tips

- Science cards compound explosively; 3 full sets plus a triple of one symbol scores 54+ points.
- Resource production in Age I pays dividends for the entire game.
- Watch your neighbors boards to predict which cards they pass vs. bury.
