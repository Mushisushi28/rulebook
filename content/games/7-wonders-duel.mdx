## Setup

Shuffle all 23 Age I cards and arrange them in the prescribed pyramid layout (some face-up, some face-down). Each player receives a player board. Lay out 4 Wonder cards and offer the snake-draft: Player A takes 1, Player B takes 2, Player A takes 1. Prepare Age II and III layouts.

---

## Gameplay

The game is played over 3 Ages. In each Age, players alternate taking cards from the card structure.

**Available cards:** Only cards fully exposed (no card beneath them) may be taken.

**On your turn, take one available card and either:**
- **Build it** in your city — pay its cost (resources or coins). Resources are produced by cards already in your city; missing resources cost coins (base price 2 + opponent production of that resource).
- **Discard it** — gain coins equal to 2 + the number of commercial structures (yellow cards) in your city.
- **Build a Wonder** — use the card as a construction token; pay the Wonder cost to complete it.

**Ages:** After all cards are taken in an Age, clear the layout and set up the next.

**Three victory conditions (instant win):**
1. **Military supremacy:** Advance the Conflict Pawn to your opponent side. If it reaches their capital (9th space), you win immediately.
2. **Scientific supremacy:** Collect 6 different Science symbols — win immediately.
3. **Civilian victory:** At game end, highest Prestige Points total wins.

**Military:** Some cards show Shields. Compare shield totals; the leader moves the Conflict Pawn 1 step toward the opponent per shield advantage (or per new shield card). Pawn position triggers Treasury raids at milestones.

---

## Winning

If no instant victory occurs, count Prestige Points at end of Age III: civilian cards, wonders, coins (3 coins = 1 VP), military bonus, guild cards. Highest total wins.

---

## Tips

- Block your opponent from powerful chains by discarding their setup cards.
- Military pressure forces resource spending on shields rather than development.
- Scientific cards snowball — react quickly if opponent collects 4 different science symbols.
