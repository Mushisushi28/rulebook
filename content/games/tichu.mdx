## Setup

Use a standard 52-card deck plus 4 special cards: Dragon (highest single), Phoenix (wild single, worth 0.5), Mahjong (lowest, leads first trick with a wish), and Dog (passes lead to your partner). Partners sit across from each other. Deal all 56 cards (14 per player).

---

## Calling Tichu

Before playing, players who believe they can go out first (empty their hand) may call Tichu (worth +100 points) or Grand Tichu (worth +200, called after seeing only first 8 cards, before seeing remaining 6). If you call and succeed, your team gains the points; if you fail (anyone goes out before you), your team loses those points.

---

## Passing

Before play, each player passes 1 card to each other player (right, left, partner) for a total of 3 cards passed.

---

## Gameplay

The player holding the Mahjong leads the first trick. Play goes in climbing order: you must beat the previous play with a higher combination of the same type. Legal plays: single cards, pairs, triples, full houses (pair + triple), straights (5+ consecutive cards of any suit), four-of-a-kind (bomb), straight flush (bomb).

**Bombs:** Four-of-a-kind or straight flush can be played anytime (out of turn) to beat any non-bomb combination. The Dragon is the highest single but is beaten by any bomb.

When no one wants to play higher, the player who played last wins the trick and leads next. The Mahjong allows the leader to wish for a specific card rank; the next player who can legally play that rank must do so.

**Dragon:** Beats everything as a single. Trick won with the Dragon must be given to an opponent.

**Dog:** Played as a single to pass the lead to your partner.

---

## Scoring

When 3 players have emptied their hands, the last player gives their remaining cards and the tricks they won to the opposing team. Count: Aces +10, Kings +10, Tens +10, Fives +5, others 0. Score separately per hand.

**One-Two finish:** If both partners go out before either opponent, they score +200 immediately (no card counting).

---

## Winning

First partnership to reach 1,000 points across multiple hands wins.

---

## Tips

- Call Tichu only with a strong hand that can control the lead.
- Save bombs for emergencies; using them at the wrong moment gifts lead to opponents.
- Coordinate with your partner through legal card plays and timing.
