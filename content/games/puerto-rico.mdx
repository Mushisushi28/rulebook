## Setup

Each player starts with an island board, a small supply of doubloons, and one plantation tile (colonist). Set up the shared supply of plantations, buildings, colonists, and goods barrels. Prepare shipping spaces. Place goods on the production wheel.

---

## Role Selection

In turn order, each player selects one of the available role cards and performs its action (all players may participate in varying degrees). After use, roles receive a doubloon bonus — the player who picks that role next time gets the bonus.

**Roles:**
- **Settler:** Claim a new plantation (or quarry) tile.
- **Mayor:** Take colonists from the supply; distribute to fill open circles on buildings/plantations.
- **Builder:** Construct a building by paying its cost minus quarry discounts.
- **Craftsman:** Produce goods (one per active staffed plantation/factory).
- **Trader:** Sell one good to the Trading House for doubloons.
- **Captain:** Ship goods to boats for Victory Points. You must ship if you can; partial shipments allowed.
- **Prospector (3–5 players):** Take 1 doubloon from the supply (no secondary action).

---

## Buildings

Large buildings (City Hall, Fortress, Customs House, etc.) score big VP at game end. Production buildings multiply output. Special buildings give ongoing rule modifications.

---

## Winning

When the colonist supply, any building size, or a ship fills, the round completes. Count:
- **Shipping VP:** Accumulated during play.
- **Building VP:** Printed value on owned and staffed buildings.

Highest total wins; tiebreaker: most goods + doubloons.

---

## Tips

- The Captain role is the primary VP engine — prioritize goods that ship well.
- Quarries discount buildings significantly; grabbing them early saves doubloons.
- Watch opponent strategies and choose roles that benefit you most while giving opponents the least benefit.
