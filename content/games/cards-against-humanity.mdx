## Setup

Shuffle black question/fill-in-the-blank cards and white answer cards into separate decks. Deal 10 white cards to each player. Select a first Card Czar (rotate clockwise). Players keep their white cards private.

---

## How to Play

The Card Czar draws a black card and reads it aloud. Black cards are either questions ("What is Batman's guilty pleasure?") or fill-in-the-blank statements.

All other players anonymously select the white card(s) from their hand that best complete the black card — some black cards require 2 or 3 white cards (indicated by blank count). Cards are passed face-down to the Card Czar.

The Czar shuffles the submissions and reads each combination aloud, then picks their favorite. The winning player takes the black card as a point (an "Awesome Point"). All players draw back to 10 white cards.

---

## Winning

The game ends when players agree to stop. The player with the most Awesome Points wins — though really everyone loses.

---

## Variants

**Happy Ending:** When ready to end the game, play the "Make a Haiku" black card for a poetic finale; all three lines chosen by each player.
**Rando Cardrissian:** Add a ghost player who draws random white cards each round — if Rando wins, everyone goes home in shame.

---

## Tips

- Read the room: what makes the Czar laugh wins, not what you personally find funniest.
- Save your most outrageous cards for lenient Czars; play subtler wit with reserved ones.
- Combos beat single words — even a bad white card can win if it creates an unexpected sentence.
