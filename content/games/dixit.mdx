## Setup

Shuffle the illustrated card deck and deal 6 cards to each player. Each player takes a voting board with numbered tokens. Place the scoring rabbit tokens on start of the track.

---

## How to Play

**Storyteller phase:** The active storyteller looks at their hand and chooses one card, then gives a clue — a word, phrase, sound, song, gesture, or abstract concept. Place the card face-down.

**Other players:** Each other player secretly chooses a card from their hand that best matches the clue and places it face-down.

**Voting:** Shuffle all submitted cards (storyteller's + others') and lay them face-up numbered 1–6 (or however many players). Each non-storyteller player secretly votes for which card they think is the storyteller's by placing a numbered token face-down. Reveal votes.

---

## Scoring

- **Storyteller scores 3** — but only if at least 1 and at most (N-1) players found the correct card (if all or none guess correctly, storyteller scores 0).
- **Each correct guesser scores 3.**
- **Each non-storyteller** scores 1 point for every vote their own card received (even if the storyteller's clue wasn't found by everyone).
- **Maximum 3 per round** per player.

---

## Winning

First player to reach **30 points** wins, or highest score when the deck runs out.

---

## Tips

- Clues must be ambiguous enough that some players fail, but clear enough that some succeed.
- Abstract clues work better than obvious ones — if everyone guesses right, the storyteller scores nothing.
- Read the room: play to the specific group's cultural references and imagination.
