## Setup

Place the 5x5 board between players. Each player places 4 Pawns and 1 Master in their home row (Pawns on the four outer squares, Master on the center). Shuffle the 16 movement cards and deal 2 to each player. Place the fifth card beside the board -- it is the neutral card available to neither player yet.

---

## Movement Cards

Each card shows a pattern of squares indicating how one piece may move relative to its current position. Cards are asymmetric -- they show movement patterns for both Red and Blue (rotated 180 degrees for the opposing player).

---

## Gameplay

On your turn:

1. Choose 1 of your 2 movement cards.
2. Move any one of your pieces (Pawn or Master) according to that card pattern. You may not move off the board or onto your own pieces. Moving onto an opponent piece captures it.
3. Place the used card beside the board (where the neutral card sat).
4. Pick up the neutral card -- it becomes part of your hand.

Your opponent now has access to the card you just discarded.

---

## Winning

Two ways to win:
- **Way of the Stone:** Capture your opponent Master piece.
- **Way of the Stream:** Move your own Master to your opponent Temple Arch space (the center square of their home row).

---

## Tips

- Think two moves ahead: the card you play now goes to your opponent.
- The Way of the Stream win is often faster and harder to defend against than hunting the Master.
- Symmetry in card patterns (like Tiger or Ox) behaves differently depending on orientation.
