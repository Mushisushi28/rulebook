## Setup

Each player selects an army color. Claim territories in turn order: players take turns placing one infantry piece on an unclaimed territory until all 42 territories are claimed.

**Starting armies:**
| Players | Infantry Each |
|---|---|
| 2 | 40 |
| 3 | 35 |
| 4 | 30 |
| 5 | 25 |
| 6 | 20 |

After claiming, players take turns placing remaining armies one at a time on their own territories. Shuffle the **42 Territory cards** (plus 2 Wild cards) into a draw pile.

Youngest player goes first; play proceeds clockwise.

---

## Turn Structure

Each turn has three phases:

### 1. Get and Place New Armies

Count your reinforcements:

**Territories:** 1 army per 3 territories you own (minimum 3 armies).

**Continent bonuses** (awarded if you own all territories in a continent):
| Continent | Bonus Armies |
|---|---|
| North America | 5 |
| Europe | 5 |
| Asia | 7 |
| South America | 2 |
| Africa | 3 |
| Australia | 2 |

**Card Sets:** If you have 3 Risk cards, you may (and must if you have 5+) trade them in for bonus armies:
- Infantry set: 4 armies
- Cavalry set: 6 armies
- Artillery set: 8 armies
- Mixed set (one of each): 10 armies
- Any set with a card matching a territory you own: +2 armies placed on that territory

The armies from successive card trades escalate: 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 15, then +5 per trade after.

Place all new armies on your own territories in any combination.

### 2. Attack (Optional)

You may attack from any territory you own that has 2+ armies into any adjacent enemy territory (or connected by sea lane). You must always leave at least 1 army behind.

**Dice rolls:**
- **Attacker** rolls 1–3 dice (up to the number of armies attacking, max 3).
- **Defender** rolls 1–2 dice (up to the number of armies in the territory, max 2).

Compare highest die to highest die, then second highest to second highest. **Ties go to the defender.** For each comparison, the loser removes 1 army.

**Capturing a territory:** When the defender loses their last army, the attacker **must** move at least the number of dice they rolled into the captured territory. They may move more, leaving at least 1 behind.

**Earning a card:** After your first successful capture of a turn, draw 1 Territory card. You earn at most 1 card per turn.

**Continuing:** You may attack as many times as you want in a turn.

### 3. Fortify (Optional)

Move any number of armies from one territory to one adjacent territory. Both territories must be yours. You may only fortify once per turn.

---

## Elimination

When a player loses their last territory, they are eliminated. Their Territory cards go to the player who eliminated them. If this gives that player 6+ cards, they must immediately trade in sets until they have 4 or fewer.

---

## Winning

Last player remaining on the board wins, having conquered all 42 territories.

---

## Mission Cards (Optional Variant)

Instead of global domination, each player receives a secret Mission card at the start. Examples: "Conquer North America and Africa," "Defeat the player with the red armies." First player to complete their mission wins.
