## Setup

Place the Birmingham board. Give each player their industry tiles (coal mines, iron works, cotton mills, breweries, manufactured goods, merchant tiles). Shuffle the card decks appropriately for player count. Deal cards to each player. Seed merchant spaces on the board per the setup diagram.

---

## Cards and Hand

Your hand consists of Location cards and Industry cards. To build or develop an industry, discard a card matching either the industry type or the location. To build links (canals/rails), discard any 2 cards. Cards do not represent resources -- they restrict what actions you may take.

---

## Actions (2 per turn)

**Build:** Pay the resource cost (coal and iron, sourced from any connected network) and discard a card to place an industry tile. Industries are built in stacks -- lower tiers must be built before higher.

**Network:** Pay coal (rail era also costs 1 beer per link) and discard 2 cards to build a canal (Canal Era) or 1-2 rail links (Rail Era). Links connect your cities to the merchant network.

**Develop:** Remove your lowest-tier industry tiles from the game (no build needed) to skip to higher tiers. Costs iron.

**Sell:** Sell cotton, manufactured goods, or pottery to a connected merchant space. Scoring is immediate; beer may be required. Flips the industry tile to its developed (gray) side.

**Loan:** Take money (12 pounds) and reduce your income track by 3 spaces.

---

## Eras

The game has two eras. Canal Era ends when the draw pile runs out; score and clear the board of canals (industry tiles stay). Rail Era uses remaining tiles. Final scoring after Rail Era.

**Scoring:** Count developed industry tiles and built links. Undeveloped industries score nothing.

---

## Winning

Most victory points wins.

---

## Tips

- Coal mines score only if connected and flipped -- build networks before building mines.
- Prioritize merchant connections; merchant bonuses compound across the game.
- Loans hurt income permanently; take them early when the income loss costs less.
