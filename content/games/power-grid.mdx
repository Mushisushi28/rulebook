## Setup

Choose a map and play regions (one per player + 1 for randomness). Remove power plant cards not used for the player count. Shuffle and stack the plant deck. Each player receives starting Elektros. Place resource tokens on the resource market board.

---

## Turn Structure (6 phases each round)

1. **Determine Player Order:** Ordered by number of cities connected (fewest first for turns 1–2; most first thereafter).
2. **Auction Power Plants:** Each player must buy at least one plant in step 1. The current minimum plant is always visible; auction starts at printed cost or higher. Players may pass after step 1.
3. **Buy Resources:** In reverse player order, buy coal, oil, garbage, or uranium from the resource market. Prices increase as resources deplete.
4. **Build (Connect Cities):** In reverse player order, pay to connect to new cities (plus connection cost between cities). Second player in a city pays more; third pays most.
5. **Bureaucracy:** Power plants burn fuel to power cities. Earn Elektros equal to cities you supply. Restock resources on the market.
6. **Game End Check:** If any player reaches the target number of cities, the game ends; the player who can power the most cities that round wins.

---

## Power Plants

Each plant has a resource type, capacity, and a minimum bid. Higher-numbered plants are more efficient. The plant market shows 8 available plants; after each purchase the lowest plant is removed and a new one slides in from the hidden stack.

---

## Winning

When the trigger city count is reached, the game ends immediately. Players calculate how many cities they can **actually power** (limited by fuel on hand). The player powering the most cities wins. Tiebreaker: most Elektros.

---

## Tips

- Track what fuel other players are buying — you can starve opponents' fuel supply.
- Efficient plants (less fuel per city) win the late game; build toward them.
- Expanding too fast early makes you a target; time your city growth for the final round.
