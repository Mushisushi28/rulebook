## Setup

Shuffle the window pattern cards and deal two double-sided cards to each player (4 options). Choose one side as your window. Each window has a difficulty number — take that many Favor Tokens. Take a player board. Set out 5 public Objective cards randomly. Deal each player one private Objective card (kept secret). Prepare the tool card row with 3 random Tool cards.

---

## Gameplay

The game lasts 10 rounds.

**Each round:**
1. **Draft:** Roll dice equal to (2 x players + 1). All players may see all dice.
2. **Turn order:** In turn order, each player picks ONE die from the pool and places it in their window, then the sequence reverses (snake draft).
3. **Placement rules:**
   - First die must touch a window edge or corner.
   - Each subsequent die must be adjacent (orthogonally or diagonally) to an already-placed die.
   - A die cannot be placed in a cell that specifies a different color or a higher/lower shade value.
   - No two orthogonally adjacent dice may share the same color OR the same shade number.
4. **Tools:** Spend Favor Tokens to use Tool cards (reroll dice, move placed dice, change shade values, etc.). First use costs 1 token; subsequent uses cost 2.

**Leftover dice** are placed on the round track as a record.

---

## Winning

After 10 rounds, calculate scores:
- Points from each public Objective card (e.g., sets of all 6 shades, rows of all different colors).
- Points from your private Objective card (sum of shade values of all dice matching your color).
- +1 point per unused Favor Token.
- -1 point per empty cell in your window.

Highest score wins.

---

## Tips

- Plan your window from outside edges inward — early placement decisions constrain everything else.
- Match your window constraints to the public objectives for maximum efficiency.
- Tool cards are often underused — spending Favor Tokens at the right moment can prevent costly mistakes.
