## Setup

Shuffle the 98 mystery word cards and place them in a draw pile. Give each player a mini-easel and a dry-erase marker. Choose a starting guesser.

---

## Gameplay

### Each Round

1. **The guesser** closes their eyes (or faces away).
2. **Reveal:** Flip a mystery word card. The guesser calls a number 1-5, selecting which of the five words on the card is the target. All other players see it; the guesser does not.
3. **Write clues:** All non-guessers write exactly ONE word on their easel as a clue for the mystery word.
4. **Eliminate duplicates:** Players compare clues. Any identical or too-similar clues (same root word, plural vs. singular, different language same word) are eliminated — turned face-down so the guesser cannot see them.
5. **Guess:** Show the guesser all remaining visible clues. The guesser has one attempt to guess the mystery word.
6. **Score:** Correct guess = +1 point (advance marker). Incorrect guess or skip = -1 point and reveal the mystery word. No guess = 0 (lose a card but keep the marker).

---

## Winning

Cooperative: the group earns 1 point per correct guess. At game end (13 cards = 13 rounds), consult the score table:

- 13: Legendary
- 11-12: Excellent
- 9-10: Good
- 7-8: Not bad
- Under 6: Keep practicing

---

## Tips

- Avoid obvious clues — they will likely be duplicated.
- Uncommon synonyms or associated concepts often survive elimination.
- Compound words and proper nouns count as single words.
