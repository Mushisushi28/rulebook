## Setup

A 19×19 grid board (beginners use 9×9 or 13×13). Black takes approximately 180 stones, White 181. Black plays first. In handicap games, the weaker player takes black and pre-places stones on marked star points.

---

## How to Play

Players alternate placing one stone on any empty intersection (lines cross, not squares). Stones are never moved once placed. Stones of the same color connected horizontally or vertically form a **group**. Each empty intersection adjacent to a stone or group is a **liberty**.

**Capture:** When a stone or group has no liberties (all adjacent intersections are occupied by opponent stones), it is captured and removed from the board.

**Ko rule:** You cannot immediately recapture the stone you just lost — the position cannot repeat immediately.

**Suicide rule:** You cannot place a stone that leaves your own group with zero liberties, unless it simultaneously captures opponent stones.

**Passing:** Either player may pass their turn. When both players pass consecutively, the game ends.

---

## Scoring

After the game, count territory (empty intersections surrounded only by your color) plus captured stones. **Komi:** White receives a fixed bonus (typically 6.5 or 7.5 points) to compensate for Black's first-move advantage. The player with more points wins.

---

## Tips

- Learn basic life-and-death shapes (two eyes give a group permanent life).
- Play toward the corners and edges first — territory is easier to secure there.
- Don't try to save every stone; sacrifice groups that are truly captured to gain elsewhere.
- Study fundamental patterns: ladders, nets, snapbacks, and ko fights.
