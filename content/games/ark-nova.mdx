## Setup

Each player takes a zoo map, a set of 5 action cards (Animals, Sponsors, Conservation, Build, Cards), and starting resources. Shuffle the animal and sponsor card decks. Deal cards and set up the conservation board. Place starting tiles on each zoo map.

---

## Action System

Each player has 5 action cards arranged in positions 1-5. On your turn, activate one card. A card in position 1 is weaker; move it to position 5 for maximum effect. After use, move the activated card to position 1 and slide all others right.

**Animals:** Pay money to play an animal card from your hand to your zoo map. The animal must fit in a correctly typed enclosure you have already built.

**Build:** Spend money to place enclosure, kiosk, pavilion, or other tiles on your zoo map (Tetris-style). Enclosure type must match the animals you plan to house.

**Sponsors:** Play sponsor cards for ongoing abilities, money, or special rules.

**Conservation:** Pay money and fulfill requirements to participate in conservation projects on the central board, earning conservation points.

**Cards:** Draw cards from one of the face-up display rows (reptile/mammal/bird/etc. decks) or take money/resources.

---

## Animals and Enclosures

Each animal has a size requirement and a habitat type (water, rock, savannah, aviary, etc.). You must build the appropriately typed enclosure before placing an animal. Animals score appeal immediately when placed and may provide bonuses (extra cards, money, conservation points, special abilities).

---

## Winning

The game ends when any player crosses the "break-even" point where their appeal score meets or exceeds their conservation score on the scoring track. Score is determined at game end; the player with the highest combined score wins.

---

## Tips

- Balance appeal (animal placement) with conservation (project participation) -- neglecting either delays your game-end trigger.
- Plan your zoo map layout early; poor tile placement leaves inefficient gaps.
- High-position action cards are powerful; rotate strategically to avoid being stuck with weak position-1 actions at critical moments.
