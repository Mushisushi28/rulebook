## Setup

Place the 8×8 board with a light square on each player's right. Arrange pieces on the back two rows: Rooks in corners, Knights beside them, Bishops next, then Queen on her own color, King beside her. Pawns fill the second rank. White moves first.

---

## Piece Movements

- **King:** One square in any direction.
- **Queen:** Any number of squares in any direction.
- **Rook:** Any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
- **Bishop:** Any number of squares diagonally (stays on one color).
- **Knight:** L-shape: 2 squares in one direction, 1 perpendicular (jumps over pieces).
- **Pawn:** One square forward (two from starting row); captures one square diagonally forward.

---

## Special Moves

**Castling:** The King slides two squares toward a Rook; the Rook jumps to the other side of the King. Requires neither piece has moved, no pieces between them, King is not in check and does not pass through check.

**En Passant:** If a pawn advances two squares from its start and lands beside an opponent's pawn, the opponent may capture it as if it had moved only one square — but must do so immediately on the next move.

**Promotion:** A pawn reaching the opposite back rank is immediately replaced by any piece (Queen, Rook, Bishop, Knight) of the same color — typically Queen.

---

## Winning

**Check:** The King is under attack. The player must resolve it (block, capture, or move King). **Checkmate:** The King is in check with no legal escape — the game ends. The checkmating player wins.

**Draw:** Stalemate (no legal moves, not in check), threefold repetition, 50-move rule, insufficient material, or mutual agreement.

---

## Tips

- Control the center with pawns and pieces early.
- Develop all pieces before launching attacks.
- Castle early to protect your King.
- Think before every move: "Can my opponent capture this piece?"
