## Setup

Separate the red noun cards from the green adjective cards. Shuffle each deck. Deal 7 red cards face-down to each player. Choose a first judge (rotate clockwise each round).

---

## How to Play

The judge flips over a green adjective card and reads it aloud. All other players choose one red noun card from their hand that they think best matches the adjective — aiming to be creative, funny, or persuasive — and place it face-down in front of the judge.

The judge shuffles the submitted red cards, reads them aloud (and may read the explanations on the backs), and picks their favorite. The player whose card is chosen wins the green card as a point. All players draw back to 7 red cards. The next player clockwise becomes the new judge.

---

## Winning

The first player to collect a set number of green cards wins:
- 4 players: 8 green cards
- 5 players: 7 green cards
- 6 players: 6 green cards
- 7 players: 5 green cards
- 8+ players: 4 green cards

---

## Tips

- Play to the judge's sense of humor and personality rather than to what seems objectively correct.
- Sometimes the most unexpected or absurd pairing wins.
- Keep a variety of red cards in hand for flexibility.
