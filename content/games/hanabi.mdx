## Setup

Shuffle the 50-card deck (5 colors, numbers 1-5; 1s appear 3 times, 2-4 appear twice, 5s once). Players hold their cards facing away so others see them but they do not. Hand size: 5 cards for 2-3 players, 4 for 4-5. Place 8 clue tokens and 3 fuse tokens nearby.

---

## Gameplay

On your turn, take exactly one action:

**Give a clue:** Spend 1 clue token. Point to one or more cards in another player hand and state either the color or the number. You must identify ALL cards of that color or number -- partial clues are not allowed.

**Discard a card:** Discard one card from your hand, gain 1 clue token (max 8), then draw a replacement. You may not discard if all 8 tokens are present.

**Play a card:** Play a card to the fireworks display. It must be the next number in its color sequence (1 then 2 then 3 then 4 then 5). A legal play adds it to the pile; an illegal play goes to the discard pile and costs 1 fuse token. After playing, draw a replacement.

---

## Winning

The game ends when the draw pile is exhausted (each player takes one final turn), when all three fuse tokens are lost (score of 0), or when all 25 cards are played correctly (perfect score). Rate your score: 15 is decent, 20 is beautiful, 25 is legendary.

---

## Tips

- Cluing a 1 early is high priority -- 1s start every sequence.
- Never discard a 5 if you can avoid it; losing a 5 makes one color permanently unfinishable.
- Finesse conventions: a clue on a card you already know about often signals "play this now."
