## Setup

Each player chooses one of 14 factions, each living on a specific terrain type. Place faction-matching terrain hexes across the map. Set up the 6 round scoring tiles and end-game bonus tiles. Deal 3 bonus tiles to each player (keep 1). Give each faction their unique starting buildings, workers, coins, and power tokens.

---

## Terrain and Terraforming

Each faction lives on one terrain type. To build on a hex, it must be your faction terrain. Terraform adjacent hexes by spending spades (workers converted via faction ability) -- each step toward your terrain costs 1 spade and generates 1 victory point for opponents with adjacent settlements.

---

## Structures

Build in order: Dwelling -> Trading House -> Stronghold/Sanctuary (one only) -> Temple. Structures provide worker/coin income and power. The Stronghold unlocks your faction special ability. Temples advance your cult tracks.

---

## Power System

You hold power in three bowls (I, II, III). When you receive power, move tokens from bowl I to II, then II to III. Spend power by moving tokens from III back to I. Power fuels special actions or buys resources cheaply.

---

## Round Structure

Each round (6 total), players take actions one at a time in turn order until all pass:
- Terraform and/or Build
- Upgrade a structure
- Send a priest to a cult track
- Advance power action
- Pass (take the next bonus tile)

Gain income at round start based on structure count and tile bonuses.

---

## Winning

After 6 rounds, score remaining resources, cult bonuses, and connected building networks (largest connected group of buildings scores 18/12/6/... points). Most points wins.

---

## Tips

- Cult tracks provide end-game points and mid-game power; neglect none entirely.
- Network bonus rewards expansive building -- avoid clustering tightly.
- Each faction has a radically different optimal strategy; learn one faction before branching out.
