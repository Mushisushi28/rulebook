## Overview

Mysterium Park is a shorter, more accessible standalone in the Mysterium universe. The ghost gives vision cards instead of speaking; investigators must interpret surreal imagery to identify the correct suspect and crime location within a tighter timeframe.

---

## Setup

Shuffle and deal 6 Suspect cards and 6 Crime Location cards in two rows. One player is the Ghost and receives the full vision card deck. Remaining players are Investigators. The Ghost secretly draws one Suspect card and one Crime Location card — these are the culprit and scene.

The game plays over 3 rounds (not 7 like the original Mysterium).

---

## Gameplay

### Each Round

1. **Ghost gives visions:** The Ghost deals 2 vision cards face-up to each Investigator (from their hand of 7+).
2. **Investigators discuss:** Each Investigator examines their pair of vision cards and announces which Suspect (and eventually Crime Location) they believe is being indicated.
3. **Reveal:** The Ghost reveals whether each Investigator guessed correctly. Correct guessers eliminate a card from their row (narrowing possibilities).
4. **Ghost replenishes hand** to 7 cards.

**Round 1:** Identify the Suspect.
**Round 2:** Identify the Crime Location.
**Round 3 (Final):** The Ghost selects one vision card for each Investigator who failed; together, the group votes on the final culprit and scene.

---

## Winning

Cooperative: if the group correctly identifies the Suspect and Crime Location in the final vote, everyone wins. If not, the culprit escapes.

---

## Tips

- Vision cards are abstract — focus on symbolic connections, not literal interpretation.
- The Ghost should eliminate cards that make the remaining target more obvious.
- Discuss eliminated possibilities openly — the Ghost can react with positive gestures to helpful insights.
