## Setup

One player takes the USA, the other USSR. Set up influence markers per the starting positions shown in the rulebook across Europe, Asia, and the Middle East. Shuffle the Early War deck. Set the Victory Point track to 0 (center). Set the DEFCON track to 5.

---

## Turn Structure

Each turn (10 total: Early War rounds 1–3, Mid War rounds 4–7, Late War rounds 8–10):

1. Improve DEFCON by 1 (max 5).
2. Deal cards (6 or 8 per round based on era).
3. Alternate headline phase: each player plays one card face-down simultaneously for its event; reveal and resolve (USSR first if tied).
4. Six action rounds: each player plays one card per round for one of:
   - **Operations:** Use the card's Ops value to place or realign influence, or attempt Coups (DEFCON dependent).
   - **Event:** Trigger the card's event text.
   - **Space Race:** Advance on the Space Race track for VPs and special abilities.

**DEFCON:** Coups in sensitive regions lower DEFCON. If DEFCON reaches 1, **thermonuclear war** — the active player loses immediately. Never trigger your own Defcon drop to 1.

---

## Victory Points

Score VP from region scoring cards, events, and space race. USSR scores from negative track (-20), USA from positive (+20). If either player reaches ±20, they win immediately.

---

## Winning

Reach 20 VP, trigger opponent's nuclear war, or hold more VP at game end (after Late War scoring).

---

## Tips

- You must play every card in your hand each round — managing opponent events is as important as your own strategy.
- Control battleground countries in each region for scoring.
- The Space Race provides important VP and DEFCON shields — don't ignore it.
