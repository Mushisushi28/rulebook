## Setup

Give each player a cauldron board, a starting set of ingredient chips (mostly white cherry bombs plus a few others), and a starting score/gold. Shuffle each player personal bag. Set up the ingredient card display for each color. Place rubies and droplets as instructed.

---

## Brewing Phase

All players brew simultaneously. Draw chips one at a time from your bag and place them in your cauldron (the spiral track, filling from center outward). After each draw, decide: stop now or keep drawing.

**White cherry bombs:** The key risk factor. The sum of all white cherry bomb values in your cauldron must not exceed 7. If it does, your cauldron explodes. You must then choose: take gold OR take victory points -- not both.

If you stop before exploding, take both gold and victory points based on how far your chip trail extends.

---

## Shopping and Upgrading

After brewing, players use earned gold to buy new ingredient chips from the display. Each color provides a different special rule effect:

- Red: Move the chip placement further ahead immediately when drawn.
- Yellow: Gain gold when drawn.
- Green: Let you draw 2, put 1 back.
- Blue: Multiply the point value of adjacent chips.
- Orange: Place a second chip when drawn.
- Purple: Score for the number of different colors in your cauldron.

---

## Evaluation and Scoring

After each round, advance on the score track. Score points based on cauldron progress. Use rubies to pull chips from the scoring track back into your bag or buy a fortune teller card. After 9 rounds, the player with the most points wins.

---

## Tips

- Buying whites early is a trap; add colored ingredients as fast as possible.
- Each color synergizes with others; build a coherent chip engine.
- When ahead in score, exploding occasionally to stockpile gold is a valid strategy.
