## Setup

Place the board in the center of the table. Divide players into 2 or 3 equal teams. Each team takes chips of one color. Shuffle the two standard playing card decks (no Jokers) together. Deal cards to each player:

| Players per team | Cards each |
|---|---|
| 1 | 7 |
| 2 | 7 |
| 3 | 6 |
| 4+ | 5 |

Each team places chips in front of them. Choose a team to go first.

---

## Gameplay

The board shows each playing card twice (except Jacks), arranged in a 10x10 grid. The four corner spaces are free spaces — treated as belonging to all teams.

On your turn:
1. Play a card from your hand face-up on the discard pile.
2. Place one of your team chips on any matching space on the board.
3. Draw a card to refill your hand.

**Jacks are wild:**
- **Two-Eyed Jacks:** Place a chip on ANY open space on the board.
- **One-Eyed Jacks:** Remove any single opponent chip from the board (cannot remove a chip in a completed sequence).

**Sequences:** Five chips of the same color in a row (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) form a sequence. Place a sequence marker on those chips. A chip in a completed sequence cannot be removed by a One-Eyed Jack.

---

## Winning

- **2 teams:** First team to complete 2 sequences wins.
- **3 teams:** First team to complete 1 sequence wins.
- **Dead cards:** If you hold a card where both matching spaces are already covered (not by you), discard the dead card on your turn and draw a replacement.

---

## Tips

- Use Two-Eyed Jacks to complete your sequence in a single turn when you have four in a row.
- Save One-Eyed Jacks to break up near-complete opponent sequences.
- Shared corner spaces are valuable — sequences running through them are easier to complete.
