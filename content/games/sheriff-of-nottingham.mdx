## Setup

Shuffle the market cards and deal 6 to each player. Place the starting gold coins. Choose a starting Sheriff. The game lasts 2 full rotations of the Sheriff role (each player is Sheriff twice in a 3-4 player game).

---

## Gameplay

Each round, one player is the Sheriff and all others are merchants.

### Merchant Phase

Each merchant selects 1-5 cards from their hand and places them face-down in their merchant bag. They declare a quantity and type to the Sheriff: "I have 3 Apples." They may only declare one type of legal good (not contraband). They may lie about quantity, type, or whether contraband is included.

### Sheriff Phase

The Sheriff may inspect any merchant bag or let it through:

**Let it through:** The merchant takes their bag back and scores the goods inside.

**Inspect:** Open the bag and examine all cards:
- If the merchant lied (wrong type or quantity), the merchant pays the penalty for each illegal card found.
- If the merchant told the truth, the Sheriff pays the merchant a penalty for wrongful search.

**Negotiation:** Before inspecting, merchants and the Sheriff may negotiate bribes (coins, promises of future cards, favors). Any deal made is binding if both agree before the bag is opened.

---

## Cards

**Legal goods:** Apples, Cheese, Bread, Chicken — scored by number in possession at end.

**Contraband:** Pepper, Mead, Silk, Crossbow — high value but illegal. Can only be smuggled, not declared.

**Special goods:** Some cards have unique abilities (adjust scoring, block inspection, etc.).

---

## Winning

After all Sheriff rotations, score goods and penalties. The player with the most gold wins. Bonus coins are awarded for the most Apples, Cheese, Bread, and Chicken cards respectively.

---

## Tips

- A consistent truth-teller is harder to read, making occasional lies more effective.
- Overbribing signals something valuable is inside.
- Pay attention to what opponents score to block critical bonuses.
