## Overview

Twilight Imperium 4th Edition is one of the most complex and celebrated strategy games ever made. Players control alien races competing to accumulate 10 Victory Points through military conquest, diplomatic maneuvering, economic development, and political agenda manipulation.

---

## Setup

Assemble the modular galaxy map from hex system tiles (variable or preset). Each player selects a faction and sets up their home system, fleet, and technology. Shuffle the Strategy, Action, Political, and Agenda card decks. Distribute Trade Goods and Command Tokens.

---

## Round Structure

Each round consists of six phases:

### 1. Strategy Phase
In clockwise order, each player selects one Strategy Card. These cards (Trade, Technology, Politics, Imperial, etc.) grant initiative order for the round and a unique primary and secondary ability.

### 2. Action Phase
Players alternate taking actions (or passing) until all have passed:
- **Tactical action:** Activate a system (place Command Token), move fleets in, potentially combat, produce resources or influence.
- **Component action:** Use a specific card or faction ability.
- **Pass:** Remove yourself from the action rotation for the round.

**Space combat:** Attacker and defender roll dice (each ship type has a combat value). Compare; ships at or above their value score a hit. Sustain damage or assign casualties.

**Planetary control:** Exhaust planets to spend resources (build units) or influence (vote on agendas or take actions).

### 3. Agenda Phase (Rounds 2+)
Draw and vote on two Agenda cards from the Agenda deck. Players spend influence to vote. Winning side resolves the agenda effect (laws, political changes, faction bonuses).

### 4. Status Phase
- Score any Public Objective cards met (revealed each round from the Objective deck).
- Score any completed Secret Objective.
- Qualify for other scoring conditions.
- Ready exhausted cards, repair units, return Command Tokens.

### 5. Agenda Phase
Occurs between Status and Strategy only in rounds with political agendas — varies by round.

---

## Victory Points

Public Objectives are revealed one per round; players who meet their condition score VPs. Secret Objectives are dealt privately and scored confidentially. Strategy Cards like Imperial grant VPs for controlling the center planet (Mecatol Rex). First to 10 VP wins immediately.

---

## Tips

- Winning requires balancing military power with political influence — ignoring either is fatal.
- Trade and Technology cards synergize; tech snowballs significantly.
- Watch the VP track carefully — when a player is at 8-9, the entire table must respond.
