## Setup

Players select characters and take their corresponding miniature, ability card deck, attack modifier deck, and character sheet. Set up the dungeon tiles and monster standees per the current scenario book. Each round, players secretly select 2 ability cards from their hand.

---

## Initiative and Turn Order

Each ability card has a top and bottom half, and an initiative number. Players reveal their chosen cards simultaneously. Turn order is determined by the lower initiative number of the two selected cards. Monsters act on printed initiative values.

---

## Player Turn

On your turn, play one ability card for its top half and the other for its bottom half (or both tops, both bottoms -- you choose which half to use from each card). Top halves are usually attacks; bottom halves usually movement, though many exceptions exist.

**Attacks:** Roll attack modifier (flip from your deck). Monsters have armor and abilities. Resolve damage, apply conditions (poison, stun, disarm, etc.).

**Long Rest:** Instead of playing cards, rest to recover your discard pile -- lose 1 card permanently (lost cards are gone for the scenario). Recover all others.

When your hand drops too low to play two cards without resting, you must rest or exhaust.

---

## Monsters

Monsters have normal and elite variants, act on initiative order, and follow simple AI rules printed on monster ability cards (drawn each round for each monster type). Monsters target the closest, most-wounded character unless otherwise noted.

---

## Winning

Each scenario has a specific objective (kill the boss, reach the exit, escort a figure, etc.). Complete it to win the scenario. Failing (all players exhausted before objective completion) means replaying from the start.

---

## Legacy Mechanics

Unlock new scenarios, retire characters when they fulfill their goal, open sealed boxes for new content, apply stickers to the city map permanently. Decisions made mid-campaign are irreversible.

---

## Tips

- Coordinate card plays with teammates -- loss cards are precious.
- Never exhaust unless the scenario is nearly won.
- Poison on enemies before large AoE attacks multiplies damage dramatically.
