## Setup

Place the board flat. Each player chooses a pawn and places it off the board (before square 1). Set the spinner in the center. The youngest player goes first.

---

## Gameplay

On your turn, spin the spinner (or roll a die in some editions) and move your pawn forward by that number of squares. Squares are numbered 1 through 100 in a winding path.

**Ladders:** If you land on the bottom of a ladder, immediately climb to the square at the top. Ladders represent good deeds and carry you forward.

**Chutes:** If you land on the top of a chute (slide), immediately slide down to the square at the bottom. Chutes represent bad behavior and set you back.

**Square 100:** You must land exactly on square 100 to win. If your spin would take you past 100, count backward by the excess number.

---

## Winning

The first player to land exactly on square 100 wins.

---

## Tips

This is a pure luck game intended for young children. The chutes and ladders mechanics intuitively illustrate cause and effect: good actions help you, bad actions hurt you. Parental engagement -- discussing why the chutes and ladders happen based on the illustrations -- enhances the learning aspect of the game.
