## Setup

Place the betting mat in the center of the table. Each player (or team) receives a whiteboard, a dry-erase marker, and 10 poker chips. Players also receive a personal betting line card. Shuffle the trivia question cards and place them in a draw pile.

---

## Gameplay

The game is played over 7 rounds.

**Question Phase:** The question reader flips a card and reads a trivia question aloud. All questions have numerical answers (examples: "What year was the Great Wall of China completed?", "How many calories are in a Big Mac?").

**Answer Phase:** All players simultaneously write down their best guess on their whiteboard. Reveal all answers at once.

**Betting Phase:** Arrange all unique answers in order from smallest to largest on the betting mat. Players now secretly place their chips on the answer they think is closest to (but not over) the correct answer. You may split chips between multiple answers. You may also bet on your own answer.

**Resolution:** Reveal the correct answer. The answer closest without going over is the winner. Players who bet on the winning answer collect their chips multiplied by the payout (higher payouts for longer-shot guesses).

**Special bets:** Each player has one "Big Payout" chip that can be placed for a larger multiplier on a single round.

---

## Scoring

After 7 rounds, the player with the most chips wins. The house (bank) covers all payouts — players do not pay each other directly.

---

## Notes

You never need to know trivia facts to win at Wits & Wagers. Recognizing which player in your group tends to know history, sports, science, etc. and betting on their answer is a fully viable strategy.
