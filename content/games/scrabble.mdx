## Setup

Place the board in the center. Put all 100 tiles in the bag and shake. Each player draws 7 tiles (do not show opponents). Youngest player goes first, or highest-scoring opening word goes first (tiles are returned and redrawn).

---

## Tile Values

Letters are worth 1–10 points. High-value tiles: Q=10, Z=10, J=8, X=8, K=5. Blank tiles are worth **0 points** but can represent any letter once played (their designation is fixed after placement).

---

## Premium Squares

The board has colored premium squares:
- **DL (Double Letter Score)** — that tile's value is doubled
- **TL (Triple Letter Score)** — that tile's value is tripled
- **DW (Double Word Score)** — the entire word's value is doubled (applied after letter premiums)
- **TW (Triple Word Score)** — the entire word's value is tripled (applied after letter premiums)

Premium squares apply **only when first covered** — they do not activate again for future words crossing that square.

If a word covers multiple word-premium squares, multiply the value multiple times (e.g., DW + TW = 6x the base value).

**The center square** (a DW star) must be covered by the first word of the game.

---

## Playing a Word

On your turn:
1. **Place tiles** on the board to form one or more words.
2. **Score** all words formed.
3. **Draw** tiles to replenish to 7.

**Placement rules:**
- The first word must cover the center star and contain at least 2 letters.
- All subsequent words must include at least one tile placed **adjacent to an existing tile**.
- New tiles must be placed in a single straight line (horizontal or vertical).
- All letter groups formed by the new tiles (in both directions) must be valid words.
- A word may be extended by adding letters to its beginning or end.
- A word may be formed by crossing through existing tiles (using them as letters in the new word).

---

## Scoring

Sum the value of every new word formed (including cross-words accidentally formed by your placement). Apply premium squares for newly placed tiles.

**Bingo:** Playing all 7 tiles in one turn earns a **50-point bonus** on top of the word scores.

---

## Challenging

Any player may challenge a word before the next player draws tiles. If the word is invalid (not in the dictionary being used), the tiles are returned and the player loses their turn. If the word is valid, the challenger loses their next turn. (Alternate rule: no penalty for challenging.)

---

## Passing and Trading

Instead of playing, a player may:
- **Pass** — skip your turn (no tiles drawn, no points)
- **Exchange** — place any number of tiles face-down, draw that many replacements, then shuffle your traded tiles back. You may only exchange if the bag has 7+ tiles.

**Game-ending pass rule:** If all players pass consecutively twice (each player passes twice in a row), the game ends.

---

## Game End

The game ends when:
- A player plays all their tiles **and the bag is empty**, OR
- All players pass consecutively (see above rule)

**Final scoring:**
- Each player subtracts the sum of their unplayed tiles from their score.
- The player who emptied their rack **adds** the sum of all other players' unplayed tiles to their score.

---

## Winning

Highest total score wins.
