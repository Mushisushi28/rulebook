## Overview

Wingspan: Nesting is a simplified standalone version of Wingspan designed to be learned in 15 minutes and played in under an hour. It retains the core Wingspan experience — drafting birds, collecting food, laying eggs, building engine chains — while removing the more complex mechanics from the base game.

---

## What Is Different

- No end-of-round goals: scoring is simpler and more direct.
- Food collection is streamlined with a simplified birdfeeder.
- The bird deck contains 140 curated birds with clearer, simpler abilities.
- Player boards are simplified with fewer symbols and clearer icons.
- Rule exceptions on individual bird cards are reduced.

---

## Core Rules (same as base Wingspan)

**Habitats:** Forest (food), Grassland (eggs), Wetland (birds). On each turn, activate one habitat.

**Playing Birds:** Pay the food and egg cost shown on the card. Place the bird in the correct habitat row.

**Powers:** Pink powers activate when any player uses that habitat. Brown powers activate only on your own turn.

**Eggs:** Eggs stored on birds at end of game score 1 point each.

**Tucked Cards and Cached Food:** Some birds tuck cards or cache food — these score at game end.

---

## Setup

Shuffle the bird deck. Deal 5 cards to each player; they keep 3. Deal 5 bonus cards; each player keeps 1. Each player receives starting food and eggs per the simplified chart.

---

## Winning

After 4 rounds, score: birds + bonus cards + end-of-round goals + eggs + tucked cards + cached food. Most points wins.
