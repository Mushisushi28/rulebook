## Setup

Shuffle the 48 dominoes and stack them face-down. Reveal 4 (or 3 for 2-player) dominoes from the draw pile and sort them by number (lowest to highest). Each player places their castle tile as the center of their future kingdom. Give each player a king token.

---

## Gameplay

**Each round:**
1. Reveal new dominoes (same count as players), sorted low to high.
2. In order, each player places their king on a newly revealed domino — first pick is the lowest, highest tile last.
3. Then, in order of the domino number they previously picked (lower = earlier), each player:
   a. Places their claimed domino into their kingdom.
   b. Moves their king to a domino in the new row.

**Placement rules:**
- Your kingdom must stay within a 5x5 grid.
- Each new domino must connect to your castle OR to at least one matching terrain type already in your grid.
- If a domino cannot legally be placed, discard it.

**Terrain types:** Wheat field, forest, sea, grassland, swamp, mine. Each has different crown distribution.

---

## Scoring

After all rounds (7 per player for 4 players), score your kingdom:

For each connected terrain group: count the tiles in the group x the crowns in the group = points.

The castle itself is neutral; it connects all terrain but scores nothing alone.

---

## Winning

Highest score wins. Tiebreaker: most tiles in a single terrain group, then most crowns.

---

## Tips

- Mine tiles (dark gray) are rare but have the most crowns — prioritize them.
- Keep your kingdom expandable; painting yourself into a corner wastes the 5x5 grid.
- Draft order matters: take a low tile to pick early next round, or a high tile for better terrain now.
