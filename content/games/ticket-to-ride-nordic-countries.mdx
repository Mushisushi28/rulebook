## Setup

Lay out the Nordic Countries game board showing Norway, Sweden, Denmark, and Finland. Shuffle the train route cards and deal 3 to each player. Players keep at least 2 routes (return excess). Each player receives 45 colored train pieces and 4 train cars to start. Shuffle the train car cards, deal 4 to each player, and place 5 face-up in the market with one face-down draw pile.

---

## Gameplay

Nordic Countries follows the same core rules as Ticket to Ride:

On your turn, take one of three actions:
1. **Draw 2 train car cards** from the face-up market or face-down deck.
2. **Claim a route** by playing matching colored cards equal to the route length and placing your train pieces on the board.
3. **Draw 3 destination tickets** (route cards) and keep at least 1.

**Tunnels:** Many routes in this map are Tunnels. When claiming a Tunnel route, after playing your cards, flip 3 additional cards from the deck. For each card of the same color as your played cards, you must pay one additional train car card. If you cannot or choose not to pay, take your cards back and your turn ends without claiming the route.

**Ferries:** Some water routes require a locomotive (wild) card as part of the payment.

---

## Scoring

Route lengths score the same as the original game. Completed destination tickets add their printed value; incomplete tickets subtract that value. The player who finishes their trains first earns a bonus 10 points.

---

## Winning

The player with the most points after final scoring wins.

---

## Notes

Nordic Countries is designed for exactly 2 or 3 players and plays faster and more cutthroat than larger-map versions. The tunnel mechanic adds significant risk to long route claims.

*Ticket to Ride: Nordic Countries is published by Days of Wonder.*
