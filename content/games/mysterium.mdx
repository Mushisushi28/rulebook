## Setup

One player is the Ghost; all others are Psychics. Lay out the Suspect, Location, and Weapon cards in separate rows (the number depends on player count and difficulty). The Ghost secretly assigns each Psychic a unique combination: one suspect, one location, one weapon. The Ghost draws a starting hand of 7 vision cards (abstract, dreamlike artwork).

---

## Gameplay

The game lasts 7 rounds (7 hours of in-game time). Each round:

**Ghost phase:** The Ghost silently deals vision cards to each Psychic -- one or more cards meant to evoke the Psychic assigned suspect (or location or weapon, depending on which phase the Psychic is in). The Ghost may not speak, gesture, or make noise.

**Psychic phase:** Each Psychic discusses with others what they think the cards mean and places their colored token on a guess. The Ghost reveals whether each Psychic is correct using a progress board.

A correct guess advances the Psychic to the next phase (suspect then location then weapon). Once all three are solved, the Psychic is done guessing. The Ghost draws replacement cards after each round.

**Final Vote:** In the final round, the Ghost sends one set of vision cards to all Psychics simultaneously. Each votes for which suspect-location-weapon combination they believe is the ghost murder. Players who solved their mystery earlier get more Clairvoyancy points (better voting accuracy). The group wins if the correct combination receives the majority vote.

---

## Winning

Cooperative: all players win if the group correctly identifies the ghost murder combination. All lose if the vote is wrong or time runs out.

---

## Tips

- Ghost: use the most striking visual element of a card, not the overall mood.
- Psychics: wild interpretations beat safe ones -- trust your instincts.
- Discuss openly; shared reasoning often unlocks the ghost intent.
