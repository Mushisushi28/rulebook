## Setup

Set up the harbor board with starting supply spaces. Lay out the starting buildings. Shuffle the building card stacks (separate by era). Give starting resources and francs to each player. Deal starting hand cards and set up the offer spaces.

---

## Gameplay

Each round, players alternate turns. On your turn, do one of two things:

**Take goods:** Take all goods from one of the offer spaces on the harbor. Offer spaces accumulate goods at the start of each round based on a schedule.

**Use a building:** Either a town building (available to all) or a building you own. Pay an entry fee (francs or goods) if it is owned by another player. Buildings allow you to process raw goods into refined goods, earn francs, take loans, construct ships, or gain other advantages.

**Feeding workers:** At certain intervals (after every 7 turns), all players must feed their workers. Pay food equal to the number of workers using bread or fish. Failing means taking a loan (each loan costs francs each round).

---

## Constructions and Ships

Spend goods (wood, clay, iron) to construct buildings and ships from the available building cards. Owning buildings earns entry fee income from other players. Ships reduce feeding costs and can be used for scoring or protection from debt.

---

## Winning

The game ends after a set number of rounds (based on player count). Players repay all loans. The richest player (total value of buildings, ships, goods, and francs) wins.

---

## Tips

- Process raw goods into refined goods for exponential value gains.
- Owning the right buildings converts entry fees into passive income.
- Plan feeding around your worker count; unexpected loan spirals are game-losers.
