## Setup

Each player begins with a wooden hut of 2 rooms and 2 family members. Deal 7 Occupation cards and 7 Minor Improvement cards to each player (E-deck for beginners, I-deck for advanced). Place action spaces on the main board; many are revealed each round. Set up the harvest schedule.

---

## Round Structure

Each of the 14 rounds:

1. **Reveal new action card** (adds one action space).
2. **Replenish resources** on accumulating spaces (wood, clay, reed, stone, food, grain, vegetables, animals).
3. **Player turns:** Each family member (worker) is placed on one available action space to take its action. Once placed, the space is blocked for that round.
4. **Harvest** (at the end of rounds 4, 7, 9, 11, 13, 14): Grow crops, breed animals, feed family (2 food per member, or 1 for a newborn).

**Actions include:** Gathering resources, plowing fields, sowing crops, building rooms and stables, renovating hut (wood → clay → stone), acquiring animals, taking loans ("begging cards"), playing Occupation/Improvement cards.

---

## Family Growth

Play a "Family Growth" action to add a new family member — but only if you have a spare room (rooms ≥ members + 1). More workers = more actions per round, but more mouths to feed.

---

## Winning (Scoring)

After round 14, score multiple categories:
- **Fields, Pastures, Grain, Vegetables, Sheep, Boar, Cattle:** Each on a scale of 0–4 points.
- **Rooms:** 1 pt each (clay) or 2 pts each (stone).
- **Family Members:** 3 pts each.
- **Unused Spaces:** -1 pt each.
- **Fenced Stables:** 1 pt each.
- **Improvements, Occupations:** Per card text.
- **Begging Cards:** -3 pts each.

Highest score wins; ties broken by food stored at game end.

---

## Tips

- Feeding your family is the hardest constraint — plan your food engine in the first few rounds.
- Having 3 family members by round 7 gives a major action-count advantage.
- Don't neglect improvement cards — their point values and abilities compound.
