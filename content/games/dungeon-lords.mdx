## Setup

Each player receives a dungeon board with 4 dungeon rooms. Set out the shared action track with 4 slots per action. Shuffle and place monster, trap, and room cards. Set up the two-year structure with 4 seasons per year (8 seasons total). Reveal adventurer parties for Year 1.

---

## Gameplay

Each season has a planning phase and an execution phase.

### Planning Phase

Secretly, each player places 3 order tokens on the shared action track (one at a time, in turn order). Actions include: hiring imps, collecting gold, acquiring food, expanding rooms, hiring monsters, laying traps, and casting spells. The action track has limited slots — some actions reject the last player who chose them.

### Execution Phase

Actions resolve in a fixed order. Players collect resources, build rooms, and recruit minions.

**Resources:** Gold (buy monsters/traps), Food (feed imps and monsters), Imps (workers who dig and transport).

**Dungeon building:** Rooms must be dug in a connected pattern. Rooms provide special abilities.

---

## Adventurers Attack (End of Each Year)

After 4 seasons, adventurers invade your dungeon. They enter room by room, fighting monsters and triggering traps.

- **Combat:** Each monster has attack and defense values. Adventurers have HP and fight until defeated or they reach the end of your dungeon.
- **Surviving adventurers:** Cause evil points (negative score) based on how deep they penetrated.
- **Defeated adventurers:** Provide loot, reputation bonuses, and occasionally prisoner cards.

---

## Winning

After Year 2, score:
- Evil points (positive for dungeon achievements)
- Minus evil points (for adventurer survival)
- Minus unpaid food/gold debts

Lowest total evil debt (or highest net score) wins.
