## Setup

The deck contains cards numbered 3 through 35. Remove 9 cards from the deck at random without looking at them. Shuffle the remaining 24 cards face-down as the draw pile. Give each player 11 chips (or the printed amount for your player count). No one looks at their chips publicly — keep them partially hidden.

---

## Gameplay

Flip the top card face-up in the center of the table. Players take turns in clockwise order. On your turn you must do one of two things:

**Take the card:** Place the card in front of you. Take all chips on the card. The next card is flipped up and play continues.

**Say "No Thanks!" and place 1 chip on the card.** The card stays in the center, now with one more chip on it. Play passes to the left.

This continues until someone takes the card.

---

## Scoring

At the end of the game (when all 24 cards have been taken), score your hand:

- Each card you hold costs you points equal to its face value.
- **Consecutive runs cancel out.** If you hold 14, 15, 16, only the lowest card (14) counts against you.
- Each chip you have remaining earns you -1 point (chips reduce your score).

The player with the lowest (most negative or least positive) score wins.

---

## Strategy

- Chips are precious. Spending your last chip forces you to take any card.
- Low-value cards are less painful to take; high-value cards with many chips may be worth claiming just for the chip windfall.
- Build consecutive runs deliberately. A card that extends your run costs you nothing extra.
- The 9 removed cards are unknown, so a run may be impossible to complete.
